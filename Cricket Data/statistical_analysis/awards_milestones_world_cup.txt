SECTION 4: MILESTONES AND LANDMARK ACHIEVEMENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BATTING MILESTONES IN WORLD CUP (2003-2023):

1. FIRST DOUBLE CENTURY IN WC HISTORY
   - Chris Gayle: 215 vs Zimbabwe (2015 WC) — first-ever WC double century
   
2. HIGHEST INDIVIDUAL SCORE IN WC HISTORY
   - Martin Guptill: 237* vs West Indies (2015 WC QF) — all-time record

3. FASTEST WC CENTURY
   - AB de Villiers: century off 31 balls vs West Indies (2015 WC)

4. MOST CENTURIES IN A SINGLE WC
   - Rohit Sharma: 5 centuries (2019 WC) — all-time record

5. MOST RUNS IN A SINGLE WC EDITION
   - Virat Kohli: 765 runs (2023 WC) — all-time record

6. KOHLI EQUALS SACHIN'S 49 ODI CENTURIES
   - During the 2023 WC, Kohli scored his 49th ODI century to equal Sachin Tendulkar's record
   - The century came against South Africa in the league stage
   - One of the most celebrated moments of the 2023 WC

7. HIGHEST SCORE IN A WC FINAL
   - Adam Gilchrist: 149 (2007 Final) — all-time record for a WC final innings

8. FIRST DOUBLE CENTURY WHILE CHASING IN WC
   - Glenn Maxwell: 201* vs Afghanistan (2023 WC) — first double century in a WC chase

BOWLING MILESTONES:

1. MOST WICKETS IN A SINGLE WC
   - Mitchell Starc: 27 wickets (2019 WC) — all-time record

2. BEST WC BOWLING FIGURES (2003-2023)
   - Glenn McGrath: 7/15 vs Namibia (2003 WC)

3. BEST WC SF BOWLING FIGURES
   - Mohammed Shami: 7/57 vs New Zealand (2023 WC SF)

4. ONLY BOWLER WITH TWO WC HAT-TRICKS
   - Lasith Malinga: hat-tricks in 2007 and 2011 WCs

5. 4 WICKETS IN 4 BALLS — WC RECORD
   - Lasith Malinga: vs South Africa (2007 WC) — only time in WC history

TEAM MILESTONES:

1. AUSTRALIA'S 4 WC TITLES IN 6 EDITIONS (2003-2023)
   - Won: 2003, 2007, 2015, 2023
   - The most dominant team in WC history during this period

2. INDIA'S 10-0 LEAGUE RECORD (2023 WC)
   - India won all 10 league matches — first team to do so in a 10-team round-robin format
   - But lost the final to Australia

3. AUSTRALIA'S 22-MATCH UNBEATEN STREAK (2003-2011)
   - Australia won 22 consecutive WC matches from 2003 to early 2011
   - This included the entire 2003 WC (11 wins) and 2007 WC (11 wins)
   - The streak ended when Pakistan beat them in the 2011 WC group stage

4. ENGLAND'S FIRST-EVER WC TITLE (2019)
   - Despite being the country that invented cricket, England had never won the ODI World Cup
   - They finally won in 2019 at Lord's, London (the "Home of Cricket")

SECTION 5: TOURNAMENT FORMAT HISTORY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2003 World Cup Format:
- 14 teams divided into 2 pools (Pool A: 7 teams, Pool B: 7 teams)
- Each team played 6 matches in the pool stage (round-robin within pool)
- Top 3 from each pool advanced to the Super Six stage (carried forward points from pool matches)
- Semi-finals and Final
- Teams: Australia, India, England, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, New Zealand, West Indies, Zimbabwe, Bangladesh, Kenya, Namibia, Netherlands, Canada

2007 World Cup Format:
- 16 teams divided into 4 groups of 4
- Each team played 3 matches in the group stage
- Top 2 from each group advanced to the Super Eight stage (carried forward 1 result)
- Semi-finals and Final
- Teams: Australia, South Africa, Netherlands, Scotland (Gp A); India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Bermuda (Gp B); England, New Zealand, Canada, Kenya (Gp C); Pakistan, West Indies, Ireland, Zimbabwe (Gp D)

2011 World Cup Format:
- 14 teams divided into 2 groups (Group A: 7 teams, Group B: 7 teams)
- Each team played 6 matches in the group stage (round-robin within group)
- Top 4 from each group advanced to Quarter-finals
- Quarter-finals, Semi-finals, Final
- Teams: Group A (Australia, Pakistan, New Zealand, Sri Lanka, Zimbabwe, Kenya, Canada); Group B (India, South Africa, England, West Indies, Bangladesh, Ireland, Netherlands)

2015 World Cup Format:
- 14 teams divided into 2 pools (Pool A: 7 teams, Pool B: 7 teams)
- Each team played 6 matches in the pool stage
- Top 4 from each pool advanced to Quarter-finals
- Quarter-finals, Semi-finals, Final
- Teams: Pool A (Australia, England, New Zealand, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Afghanistan, Scotland); Pool B (India, South Africa, Pakistan, West Indies, Ireland, Zimbabwe, UAE)
- Crossover QFs: A1 vs B4, A2 vs B3, A3 vs B2, A4 vs B1

2019 World Cup Format:
- 10 teams in a single round-robin league (each team played 9 matches)
- Top 4 teams qualified for Semi-finals
- Semi-finals and Final
- Teams: Australia, England, India, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, West Indies, Bangladesh, Afghanistan
- First WC since 1992 with a round-robin format

2023 World Cup Format:
- 10 teams in a single round-robin league (each team played 9 matches)
- Top 4 teams qualified for Semi-finals
- Semi-finals and Final
- Teams: Australia, England, India, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Afghanistan, Netherlands
- Same format as 2019

SECTION 7: COMPREHENSIVE Q&A FOR COMMON WORLD CUP QUESTIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: Who won the 2003 World Cup?
A: Australia beat India by 125 runs in the final at Johannesburg. Ricky Ponting scored 140*.

Q: Who won the 2007 World Cup?
A: Australia beat Sri Lanka by 53 runs in the final at Kensington Oval, Barbados. Adam Gilchrist scored 149.

Q: Who won the 2011 World Cup?
A: India beat Sri Lanka by 6 wickets in the final at Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai. MS Dhoni scored 91* and hit the winning six.

Q: Who won the 2015 World Cup?
A: Australia beat New Zealand by 7 wickets in the final at MCG, Melbourne. Mitchell Starc was Player of the Tournament.

Q: Who won the 2019 World Cup?
A: England beat New Zealand in the final at Lord's. The match was tied (241-241), the Super Over was tied (15-15), and England won on boundary count. Ben Stokes scored 84*.

Q: Who won the 2023 World Cup?
A: Australia beat India by 6 wickets in the final at Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad. Travis Head scored 137.

Q: What was the 2019 final tie-breaking rule?
A: The match was tied at 241 each after 50 overs. A Super Over was played where both teams scored 15 runs. Since the Super Over was also tied, the winner was decided by boundary count — the team that hit more boundaries in the match won. England hit 26 boundaries to New Zealand's 17. This boundary count rule was later abolished by the ICC.

Q: How did the 2019 World Cup final end?
A: The 2019 WC final is the most dramatic match in cricket history. NZ scored 241/8 batting first. England scored 241 all out to tie. In the Super Over, England scored 15/0 (Stokes & Buttler), NZ scored 15/1 (Guptill run out on last ball by Buttler). Both Super Over scores tied at 15. England won on boundary count (26 vs 17 boundaries). The overthrow incident where a throw hit Stokes' bat and went for 4 was highly controversial.

Q: Who is the highest run-scorer in World Cup history (2003-2023)?
A: Virat Kohli with 1638 runs in 27 matches at an average of 78.00.

Q: Who is the highest wicket-taker in World Cup history (2003-2023)?
A: Mitchell Starc with 67 wickets in 26 matches at an average of 17.09.

Q: What is the highest individual score in World Cup history?
A: Martin Guptill scored 237* vs West Indies in the 2015 WC quarter-final at Wellington.

Q: What is the highest team score in World Cup history (2003-2023)?
A: Australia scored 417/6 vs Afghanistan in the 2015 WC at Perth.

Q: What is the fastest century in World Cup history?
A: AB de Villiers scored a century off 31 balls vs West Indies in the 2015 WC at Sydney.

Q: Has any team scored over 400 in a World Cup match?
A: Yes. Australia scored 417/6 vs Afghanistan (2015), India scored 413/5 vs Bermuda (2007), South Africa scored 411/4 vs Ireland (2015), and South Africa scored 408/5 vs West Indies (2015).

Q: Which team has the most World Cup titles (2003-2023)?
A: Australia with 4 titles (2003, 2007, 2015, 2023). They won 4 out of 6 World Cups in this period.

Q: Who was the 2019 World Cup runner-up?
A: New Zealand were the runners-up in the 2019 World Cup. They lost the final to England via boundary count after both the match and Super Over ended in ties.

Q: Who was the 2023 World Cup runner-up?
A: India were the runners-up in the 2023 World Cup. They lost the final to Australia by 6 wickets at Ahmedabad.
