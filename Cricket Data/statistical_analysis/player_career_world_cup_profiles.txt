──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SACHIN TENDULKAR (India) — World Cups: 2003, 2007, 2011
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall WC Career (2003-2011): 20 matches, 709 runs, Avg 47.27, SR 88.63, 2 centuries, 4 fifties, HS 152
Also played in 1992, 1996, 1999 WCs. All-time WC career: 45 matches, 2278 runs, Avg 56.95, 6 centuries, 15 fifties.
Role: Top-order batter, right-hand bat, right-arm various
All-time leading run-scorer in World Cup history (across all editions 1992-2011).

2003 World Cup (South Africa):
- Matches: 11, Runs: 673, Average: 61.18, Strike Rate: 100.75
- Centuries: 1 (152 vs Namibia), Fifties: 6 (including 98 vs Pakistan)
- Fours: 84, Sixes: 6
- Player of the Tournament award winner
- Key performances: 152 vs Namibia at Pietermaritzburg, 98 vs Pakistan at Centurion
- Milestone: Highest run-scorer of the tournament (673 runs)
- India reached the final but lost to Australia by 125 runs
- In the final vs Australia: scored 4, caught Gilchrist bowled McGrath

2007 World Cup (West Indies):
- Matches: 3, Runs: 82, Average: 27.33, Strike Rate: 72.57
- Centuries: 0, Fifties: 0
- India were eliminated in the group stage
- Lost to Bangladesh and Sri Lanka in group stage
- Scored 7 vs Bangladesh, 57 vs Bermuda, 18 vs Sri Lanka

2011 World Cup (India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh):
- Matches: 9, Runs: 482, Average: 53.56, Strike Rate: 84.12
- Centuries: 2 (120 vs England at Bangalore, 111 vs South Africa at Nagpur)
- Fifties: 2
- Fours: 46, Sixes: 5
- This was Sachin's last World Cup and India won the tournament
- In the final vs Sri Lanka: scored 18, caught Dilshan bowled Malinga
- India lifted the World Cup trophy at Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai — Sachin's home ground
- Sachin was carried on teammates' shoulders in an iconic moment

RICKY PONTING (Australia) — World Cups: 2003, 2007, 2011
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall WC Career (2003-2011): 25 matches, 1030 runs, Avg 49.05, SR 84.22, 2 centuries, 6 fifties, HS 140*
Also played in 1996, 1999 WCs. All-time WC career: 46 matches, 1743 runs, Avg 45.34, 4 centuries, 10 fifties.
Role: Top-order batter, captain (2003, 2007, 2011), right-hand bat
Won World Cup as captain in 2003 and 2007 (two consecutive titles).

2003 World Cup (South Africa):
- Matches: 11, Runs: 415, Average: 69.17, Strike Rate: 95.82
- Centuries: 1 (140* in the final vs India), Fifties: 4
- Captain of Australia; led Australia to the title
- In the final: scored 140* off 121 balls (4 fours, 8 sixes) — one of the greatest WC final innings ever
- Player of the Match in the final
- Tournament highlight: masterclass 140* demolished India's bowling attack in the final

2007 World Cup (West Indies):
- Matches: 11, Runs: 343, Average: 42.88, Strike Rate: 79.40
- Centuries: 0, Fifties: 4
- Captain: Led Australia to their 3rd consecutive WC title (and 4th overall)
- Key performance: Steady 60s and 70s throughout the tournament
- In the final vs Sri Lanka: scored 37
- Australia won the final by 53 runs at Kensington Oval, Barbados

2011 World Cup (India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh):
- Matches: 7, Runs: 261, Average: 37.29, Strike Rate: 75.43
- Centuries: 1 (104 vs India in QF), Fifties: 1
- Captain in his final WC
- Australia were eliminated in the quarter-final by India
- In the QF vs India: scored 104 off 124 balls but Australia lost
- This was Ponting's last World Cup match

MS DHONI (India) — World Cups: 2007, 2011, 2015, 2019
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall WC Career (2007-2019): 24 matches, 698 runs, Avg 46.53, SR 82.94, 0 centuries, 5 fifties, HS 91*
Role: Wicketkeeper-batter, middle-order, captain (2011, 2015, 2019), right-hand bat
Won 2011 World Cup as captain. One of the most successful WC captains in history.

2007 World Cup (West Indies):
- Matches: 3, Runs: 29, Average: 14.50, Strike Rate: 55.77
- India were eliminated in the group stage
- Lost to Bangladesh (upset) and Sri Lanka
- Dhoni was relatively new to the team at this point

2011 World Cup (India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh):
- Matches: 9, Runs: 368, Average: 52.57, Strike Rate: 87.83
- Centuries: 0, Fifties: 3
- Captain of India — led India to World Cup victory
- In the final vs Sri Lanka: scored 91* off 79 balls, hit the winning six over long-on off Nuwan Kulasekara
- That winning six is one of the most iconic moments in cricket history
- Player of the Match in the final
- Key performances: 87* vs Pakistan (SF), 91* vs Sri Lanka (Final)
- Dhoni promoted himself to bat at No. 5 ahead of in-form Yuvraj Singh in the final

2015 World Cup (Australia, New Zealand):
- Matches: 8, Runs: 225, Average: 56.25, Strike Rate: 85.23
- Centuries: 0, Fifties: 1 (65 vs West Indies)
- Captain of India
- India reached the semi-final, lost to Australia by 95 runs at Sydney
- Key performance: Steady hand in middle-order throughout the tournament

2019 World Cup (England and Wales):
- Matches: 9, Runs: 273, Average: 45.50, Strike Rate: 87.50
- Centuries: 0, Fifties: 1 (50* vs Bangladesh)
- Not captain (Virat Kohli was captain), played as senior wicketkeeper-batter
- India reached the semi-final, lost to New Zealand in a rain-affected match at Manchester
- In the semi-final vs New Zealand: scored 50 off 72 balls before being run out by Martin Guptill's direct hit
- That run-out ended Dhoni's World Cup career — an emotional moment
- This was MS Dhoni's last international match ever

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall WC Career (2011-2023): 27 matches, 1638 runs, Avg 78.00, SR 89.46, 5 centuries, 10 fifties, HS 117
Role: Top-order batter, captain (2019), right-hand bat
One of the greatest ODI batters of all time. Highest average among players with 1000+ WC runs.

2011 World Cup (India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh):
- Matches: 9, Runs: 282, Average: 35.25, Strike Rate: 86.50
- Centuries: 0, Fifties: 3
- Young Kohli played as a middle-order batsman
- In the final vs Sri Lanka: scored 35 off 49 balls
- In the QF vs Australia: scored 24
- In the SF vs Pakistan: scored 9

2015 World Cup (Australia, New Zealand):
- Matches: 8, Runs: 305, Average: 50.83, Strike Rate: 88.15
- Centuries: 1 (107 vs Pakistan at Adelaide), Fifties: 2
- Key performance: 107 vs Pakistan in the Pool B clash at Adelaide Oval
- India reached the semi-final, lost to Australia
- In the SF vs Australia: scored 1

2019 World Cup (England and Wales):
- Matches: 9, Runs: 443, Average: 55.38, Strike Rate: 88.78
- Centuries: 1 (82 vs... actually his century was 103 vs Australia at The Oval)
- Actually: scored 443 runs with 5 fifties and 1 century (103 vs Australia)
- Captain of India in this tournament
- India reached the semi-final, lost to New Zealand
- In the SF vs New Zealand: scored 1
- Key performances: 103 vs Australia, 72 vs Pakistan, 77 vs West Indies

2023 World Cup (India):
- Matches: 11, Runs: 765, Average: 95.63, Strike Rate: 90.32
- Centuries: 3, Fifties: 6
- Highest run-scorer of the tournament (765 runs)
- Player of the Tournament
- Equaled Sachin Tendulkar's record of 49 ODI centuries during this tournament
- Key performances: 117 vs New Zealand, 103* vs Bangladesh, 95 vs Australia (league)
- India won all 10 league matches but lost the final to Australia
- In the final vs Australia: scored 54 off 63 balls
- In the SF vs New Zealand: scored 117 — a match-winning knock
- Kohli's 765 runs in a single WC edition is the highest ever by any batter in a single World Cup

  140 vs Pakistan, 122* vs South Africa, 104 vs England, 102 vs Bangladesh, 103 vs Sri Lanka

- Matches: 11, Wickets: 26, Average: 13.73, Economy: 3.55, Best: 3/16

- Player of the Tournament
- Instrumental in Australia winning their 3rd consecutive World Cup
- In the final vs Sri Lanka: took 0/36 (but McGrath was economical throughout)
- McGrath retired from ODIs after this World Cup as the all-time WC leading wicket-taker

Most successful Sri Lankan bowler in World Cup history.

2011 World Cup (India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh):

- Best bowling: 6/38 vs Kenya at Colombo
- Sri Lanka reached the final, lost to India
- In the final vs India: took 2/42 (dismissed Sehwag and Sachin Tendulkar)

2015 World Cup (Australia, New Zealand):
- Matches: 7, Wickets: 13, Average: 21.85, Economy: 5.00, Best: 4/43
- Sri Lanka lost in the quarter-final to South Africa

2019 World Cup (England and Wales):
- Matches: 7, Wickets: 13, Average: 26.08, Economy: 5.91, Best: 4/43
- Sri Lanka had a disappointing tournament, did not qualify for semi-finals
- This was Malinga's last World Cup

Starc vs Malinga World Cup comparison:
- Mitchell Starc: 26 matches, 67 wickets, Avg 17.09, SR 21.3
- Lasith Malinga: 28 matches, 56 wickets, Avg 21.02, SR 24.4
- Starc has more wickets per match (2.58 vs 2.00) and a better average
- Starc has 27 wickets in a single WC (2019) — most by anyone in one edition
- Malinga was the superior death-overs bowler in terms of yorker execution
- Both are among the top 5 all-time WC wicket-takers in the 2003-2023 era

EOIN MORGAN (England) — World Cups: 2015, 2019
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overall WC Career (2015-2019): 17 matches, 547 runs, Avg 36.47, SR 91.17, 1 century, 2 fifties, HS 148
Also played for Ireland in 2007 WC. Role: Middle-order batter, captain (2015, 2019), left-hand bat.

As Captain:
- 2015 World Cup: 6 matches as captain. England had a disastrous WC, eliminated in group stage.

Morgan vs Williamson captaincy comparison:
- Eoin Morgan: 17 matches as WC captain, Won 10, Lost 7, Win% 58.8%. Won 1 World Cup (2019).
- Kane Williamson: 17 matches as WC captain, Won 9, Lost 8, Win% 52.9%. Runner-up 2019, SF exit 2023.
- Morgan had the edge with a WC title; Williamson scored more runs as captain.
- Both are regarded as among the finest limited-overs captains of their generation.
