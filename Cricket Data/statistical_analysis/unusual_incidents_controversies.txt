SECTION 1: UNUSUAL DISMISSALS IN WORLD CUP (2003-2023)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OBSTRUCTING THE FIELD:
- In the history of WC matches 2003-2023, obstructing the field dismissals are extremely rare.
- The most notable instance in ODI cricket was Steve Waugh vs Sri Lanka (1999 WC), but that was before 2003.
- During the 2023 World Cup, there were no confirmed "obstructing the field" dismissals.
- The mode "obstructing the field" has been merged with "handled the ball" under the current ICC rules (since 2017). Both are now covered under "obstructing the field."

HANDLED THE BALL:
- "Handled the ball" was a separate mode of dismissal until 2017 when the ICC merged it with "obstructing the field."
- In World Cups from 2003-2023, there were no confirmed "handled the ball" dismissals.
- The most famous "handled the ball" dismissal in ODI history was Michael Vaughan (England) vs India in 2003, but this was NOT in the World Cup — it was in a separate ODI series.
- No player was given out "handled the ball" in any WC match from 2003 to 2023.

TIMED OUT:
- No batsman has ever been timed out in a World Cup match (2003-2023).

HIT WICKET:
- Several instances of hit wicket in WC 2003-2023, but these are standard dismissals (batter dislodges bails while playing a shot).
- Example: Multiple batsmen dismissed hit wicket across the 6 WC editions.

RETIRED HURT AND RETURNED TO BAT:
- A batsman can retire hurt and return later if fit.
- In the 2003 World Cup, there were instances of batsmen retiring hurt due to injury and returning later in the innings.
- Notable instance: During the 2003 WC, Craig Wishart (Zimbabwe) retired hurt vs India but did not return.

RUN OUT OFF FREE HIT:
- A batsman can be run out off a free hit delivery (the only mode of dismissal allowed off a free hit).
- Several instances across 2003-2023 WCs.

SECTION 2: MAJOR CONTROVERSIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. 2019 WORLD CUP FINAL — THE OVERTHROW CONTROVERSY
   Match: England vs New Zealand, Final, Lord's, 14 July 2019
   What happened:
   - In the 50th over of England's innings, with England needing 9 runs to win
   - Ben Stokes hit the ball to deep mid-wicket, ran for 2
   - Martin Guptill threw the ball from the deep towards the keeper's end
   - The throw hit Stokes' outstretched bat while he was running back to his crease
   - The ball deflected off Stokes' bat and ran to the boundary
   - Umpire Kumar Dharmasena awarded 6 runs (2 completed + 4 for the overthrow)
   - Many experts argued it should have been 5 runs total:
     * The batsmen had completed 1 run and crossed for the 2nd when the throw was made
     * Under Law 19.8, the runs scored should be 1 (completed) + 4 (overthrow boundary) = 5
   - Dharmasena later admitted the decision may have been incorrect
   - This 1-run difference was crucial in a match that ended in a tie (241-241)
   - Impact: Enormous. If 5 runs were awarded instead of 6, England would have scored 240 (losing by 1 run) — New Zealand would have won the World Cup.
   - The ICC changed the overthrow laws after this incident

2. 2019 WORLD CUP FINAL — BOUNDARY COUNT RULE
   - After the match and Super Over both tied, England won on boundary count (more boundaries hit)
   - England hit 26 boundaries to New Zealand's 17 in the entire match
   - Critics argued this was an arbitrary tiebreaker
   - New Zealand were unlucky — they never actually lost in the traditional sense
   - The ICC abolished the boundary count rule after this match
   - New rule: If a Super Over is tied, another Super Over is played until there's a winner

3. 2019 WORLD CUP FINAL — DRS AND UMPIRING DECISIONS
   - The final featured several close umpiring decisions
   - The most discussed DRS moment: The overthrow wasn't a DRS review but an on-field umpiring call
   - There were also tight LBW decisions reviewed via DRS during the match
   - On-field umpire Kumar Dharmasena and Marais Erasmus officiated
   - The overall umpiring standard was scrutinized heavily after the controversial ending

4. 2003 WORLD CUP — SOUTH AFRICA'S D/L METHOD CONFUSION
   Match: South Africa vs Sri Lanka, Group B, Durban, 3 March 2003
   What happened:
   - Rain-affected match, D/L method applied
   - South Africa's Mark Boucher blocked the last ball, thinking they had enough to win
   - In reality, they needed one more run to win (they needed 229 off 45 overs via D/L)
   - The match was tied, and South Africa were eliminated on net run rate
   - South Africa's coaching staff were blamed for not communicating the D/L par score correctly
   - One of the most tragic exits in WC history

5. 2003 WORLD CUP — ENGLAND FORFEIT MATCH IN ZIMBABWE
   - England refused to play their Group A match vs Zimbabwe in Harare due to political unrest
   - The match was recorded as a forfeit — Zimbabwe got 2 points
   - This forfeit cost England qualification to the Super Six stage
   - A diplomatic/political decision that had sporting consequences

6. 2007 WORLD CUP — BOB WOOLMER'S DEATH
   - Pakistan coach Bob Woolmer was found dead in his hotel room on 18 March 2007
   - One day after Pakistan's shock group-stage loss to Ireland
   - Initially treated as a potential homicide; Jamaican police investigated
   - The case was eventually closed with the conclusion that Woolmer died of natural causes (heart failure)
   - Cast a dark shadow over the entire 2007 World Cup

7. 2007 WORLD CUP — FINAL PLAYED IN NEAR-DARKNESS
   - The 2007 Final between Australia and Sri Lanka was disrupted by rain
   - The match was played partly under fading light / in near-darkness
   - Sri Lanka's innings was reduced due to rain, played under lights
   - The match finish was somewhat anticlimactic due to poor visibility

8. 2011 WORLD CUP — COIN TOSS CONTROVERSY (INDIA vs PAKISTAN SF)
   - There were allegations (unproven) about the toss in the India vs Pakistan semi-final
   - The match was played at Mohali with enormous pressure and security

9. 2023 WORLD CUP — INDIA'S PITCH PREPARATION ALLEGATIONS
   - Some teams alleged that pitches in India were prepared to suit the host team
   - India won all 10 league matches on pitches that generally favored batting and spin
   - These allegations were denied by the BCCI and ICC

SECTION 3: DRAMATIC MATCH FINISHES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. 2019 FINAL — THE TIED MATCH AND TIED SUPER OVER
   England vs New Zealand, Lord's — Both scored 241 in 50 overs, then both scored 15 in the Super Over.
   England won on boundary count. The most dramatic finish in cricket history.

2. 2011 FINAL — DHONI'S WINNING SIX
   India vs Sri Lanka, Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai — Dhoni hit a six over long-on to win India's first WC in 28 years.

3. 2015 SF — GRANT ELLIOTT'S SIX vs SOUTH AFRICA
   New Zealand vs South Africa, Eden Park, Auckland — NZ needed 12 off 7, Elliott hit Steyn for six.
   De Villiers in tears. South Africa's WC curse continued.

4. 2023 FINAL — INDIA'S HEARTBREAK AT AHMEDABAD
   India 240 all out, Australia 241/4 (Travis Head 137). India lost after winning 10 straight matches.
   130,000 fans went silent.

5. 2007 GROUP — IRELAND BEAT PAKISTAN
   Ireland chased down the DL target to beat Pakistan. Pakistan eliminated from the group stage.
   The next day, coach Bob Woolmer was found dead.

6. 2007 GROUP — BANGLADESH BEAT INDIA
   Bangladesh beat India in the group stage, contributing to India's shock elimination.

7. 2011 GROUP — IRELAND CHASE 327 vs ENGLAND
   Kevin O'Brien's 50-ball century (113 off 63 balls) led Ireland to chase 327.

8. 2023 GROUP — MAXWELL'S 201* vs AFGHANISTAN
   Australia 91/7 chasing 292. Maxwell scored 201* despite barely being able to run.
   One of the greatest ODI innings ever.

9. 2003 SUPER SIX — SOUTH AFRICA vs SRI LANKA TIE
   The D/L confusion that eliminated South Africa. Mark Boucher blocked the last ball.

10. 2015 POOL — AFGHANISTAN vs SCOTLAND
    Afghanistan vs Scotland at Dunedin in the 2015 WC Pool A.
    This match featured Shapoor Zadran bowling aggressively.
    Misbah-ul-Haq took a hat-trick? No — this match did not feature a hat-trick.
    Note: No confirmed hat-trick occurred in the Afghanistan vs Scotland match in the 2015 WC.

SECTION 4: NOTABLE BOUNDARY CATCHES AND FIELDING INCIDENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. TRENT BOULT BOUNDARY CATCH IN 2019 FINAL
   - In the 2019 final, Trent Boult took a brilliant catch near the boundary rope
   - However, he stepped on the boundary rope while taking the catch
   - The catch was reviewed and the batsman was given NOT OUT — 6 runs awarded
   - This happened to Ben Stokes during a critical moment in the match
   - Stokes was on 46 at the time; he went on to score 84* and take England to the tied score
   - If that catch had stood, the match might have had a completely different outcome

2. JONTY RHODES TRADITION — FIELDING EXCELLENCE
   - South Africa maintained their fielding excellence tradition throughout WCs
   - AB de Villiers was an outstanding fielder in 2007, 2011, 2015

3. RAVINDRA JADEJA'S FIELDING IN 2019 AND 2023 WCs
   - Jadeja was one of the best fielders in both tournaments
   - Saved numerous runs and took spectacular catches
