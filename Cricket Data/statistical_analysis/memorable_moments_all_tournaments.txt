MEMORABLE MOMENTS AND KEY EVENTS — ALL ICC CRICKET WORLD CUPS (2003–2023)
Tournament-by-Tournament Breakdown

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
2003 ICC CRICKET WORLD CUP — SOUTH AFRICA, ZIMBABWE, KENYA
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Format: 14 teams, 2 pools (Pool A: 7 teams, Pool B: 7 teams), Super Six stage, Semi-finals, Final.
Winner: Australia. Runner-up: India.
Player of the Tournament: Sachin Tendulkar.

KEY MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

1. RICKY PONTING'S MASTERCLASS IN THE FINAL
   - Australia vs India, Final, 23 March 2003, Johannesburg (Wanderers)
   - Ponting scored 140* off 121 balls (4 fours, 8 sixes) to take Australia to 359/2
   - India were bowled out for 234, Australia won by 125 runs
   - One of the greatest final innings in WC history

2. SACHIN TENDULKAR'S 673 RUNS — PLAYER OF THE TOURNAMENT
   - Despite India losing the final, Sachin's 673 runs was the highest in a single WC at the time
   - He scored consistently throughout: 152 vs Namibia, 98 vs Pakistan, 97 vs Sri Lanka
   - Won the Golden Bat award

3. INDIA vs PAKISTAN — HIGH-VOLTAGE GROUP MATCH
   - India beat Pakistan at Centurion, Sachin scored 98
   - Most watched cricket match of 2003 WC
   - Estimated TV audience of over 500 million

4. ANDY BICHEL'S HEROICS vs ENGLAND
   - Bichel took 7/20 (best WC figures at the time for Australia in a single match) 
   - Then scored 34* to win the match for Australia
   - All-round performance that eliminated England's hopes

5. KENYA'S FAIRY TALE RUN TO THE SEMI-FINALS
   - Kenya, an associate nation, reached the semi-finals
   - Beat Sri Lanka in group stage (Jayasuriya's team)
   - Beat Zimbabwe and advanced through Super Six stage
   - Lost to India in the semi-final
   - Biggest upset story of the tournament

6. ENGLAND AND NEW ZEALAND REFUSE TO PLAY IN ZIMBABWE/KENYA
   - Due to political unrest in Zimbabwe, England forfeited their group match
   - This forfeiture ultimately contributed to England's elimination

7. SOUTH AFRICA'S TYING FIASCO vs SRI LANKA
   - Rain-affected match, South Africa needed to know D/L par scores
   - Mark Boucher blocked the last ball thinking they'd won, but needed one more run
   - South Africa tied (which was not enough to advance), eliminated from the tournament
   - One of the most dramatic and tragic exits in WC history

8. GLENN MCGRATH'S 7/15 vs NAMIBIA
   - Best bowling figures of the tournament: 7/15
   - Namibia were bowled out for 45
   - Most dominant bowling performance in the 2003 WC

9. CHAMINDA VAAS'S OPENING OVER HAT-TRICK vs BANGLADESH
   - Vaas took a hat-trick in the first over of the match
   - First 3 balls of the innings: 3 wickets
   - Finished with 6/25

10. SACHIN TENDULKAR'S DISMISSAL IN THE 2003 FINAL
    - Tendulkar was caught by Gilchrist off McGrath for just 4 runs in the final
    - It was a regulation catch behind the stumps; nothing unusual about the mode of dismissal
    - The disappointment was that India's talisman fell so cheaply in the biggest match

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
2007 ICC CRICKET WORLD CUP — WEST INDIES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Format: 16 teams, 4 groups of 4, Super Eight stage, Semi-finals, Final.
Winner: Australia (3rd consecutive WC title). Runner-up: Sri Lanka.
Player of the Tournament: Glenn McGrath.

KEY MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

1. INDIA ELIMINATED IN GROUP STAGE — SHOCKING EXIT
   - India lost to Bangladesh by 5 wickets in their opening match at Port of Spain
   - Then lost to Sri Lanka in the group stage
   - India, one of the favorites, were out before the Super Eight stage
   - This was one of the biggest shocks in WC history
   - Led to massive backlash from fans, effigies burned in India

2. PAKISTAN ELIMINATED IN GROUP STAGE
   - Pakistan also failed to advance past the group stage
   - Lost to Ireland by 3 wickets (DL method) in a massive upset
   - Two cricketing giants eliminated in the first round

3. IRELAND BEAT PAKISTAN — HISTORIC UPSET
   - Ireland vs Pakistan at Sabina Park, Kingston, Jamaica, 17 March 2007
   - Ireland chased down the DL target, winning by 3 wickets
   - This was Ireland's first-ever WC win against a Test nation
   - One of the greatest upsets in cricket history

4. BOB WOOLMER'S DEATH
   - Pakistan coach Bob Woolmer was found dead in his hotel room on 18 March 2007
   - One day after Pakistan's shock loss to Ireland
   - Initially investigated as potential murder, later ruled as natural causes (heart attack)
   - Cast a dark shadow over the entire tournament

5. ADAM GILCHRIST'S 149 IN THE FINAL
   - 2007 Final: Australia vs Sri Lanka at Kensington Oval, Barbados, 28 April 2007
   - Gilchrist scored 149 off 104 balls (13 fours, 8 sixes)
   - Highest individual score in a World Cup final (still the record as of 2023)
   - Australia won by 53 runs (final reduced due to rain)
   - Gilchrist famously used a squash ball inside his batting glove for "feel"
   - Player of the Match in the final

6. LASITH MALINGA'S 4 WICKETS IN 4 BALLS vs SOUTH AFRICA
   - Super Eight match: Sri Lanka vs South Africa at Providence Stadium, Guyana
   - Malinga took 4 wickets in consecutive deliveries — the only time this has happened in WC history
   - His victims were: Shaun Pollock, Andrew Hall, Jacques Kallis, Makhaya Ntini
   - Included a hat-trick (3 in 3) plus a 4th in the next ball
   - One of the most remarkable bowling feats in cricket history

7. BANGLADESH BEAT INDIA — UPSET OF THE CENTURY
   - Bangladesh vs India at Queen's Park Oval, Port of Spain, 17 March 2007
   - Bangladesh scored 191, then bowled India out for 175
   - India lost by 5 wickets (wait - correction: Bangladesh won by 5 wickets? Actually Bangladesh batted second)
   - Actually: India scored 191, Bangladesh chased it down. Bangladesh won.
   - Mashrafe Mortaza was Player of the Match
   - This loss, combined with the loss to Sri Lanka, eliminated India

8. AUSTRALIA'S HISTORIC 3RD CONSECUTIVE WORLD CUP TITLE
   - Australia won 2003, 2007 WCs (also won 1999)
   - 3 consecutive WC titles is a feat unlikely to be repeated
   - Under Ricky Ponting's captaincy, they were utterly dominant
   - Won all 11 matches in the 2007 tournament

9. MATTHEW HAYDEN'S 659 RUNS — TOP SCORER
   - Hayden was the highest run-scorer of the tournament with 659 runs in 11 matches
   - Scored 3 centuries including 158 vs West Indies

10. GLENN MCGRATH'S 26 WICKETS — LEADING WICKET-TAKER
    - McGrath took 26 wickets in 11 matches, economy 3.55
    - Named Player of the Tournament
    - This was McGrath's farewell WC — he retired after this tournament

11. MATCH THAT ENDED IN A TIE — SOUTH AFRICA vs SRI LANKA
    - Super Eight: South Africa vs Sri Lanka at Providence, Guyana, 2007
    - This match was tied with both teams scoring the same total
    - One of the rare tied matches in WC history

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
2011 ICC CRICKET WORLD CUP — INDIA, SRI LANKA, BANGLADESH
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Format: 14 teams, 2 groups (Group A: 7 teams, Group B: 7 teams), Quarter-finals, Semi-finals, Final.
Winner: India. Runner-up: Sri Lanka.
Player of the Tournament: Yuvraj Singh.

KEY MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

1. MS DHONI'S WINNING SIX IN THE FINAL
   - India vs Sri Lanka, Final, 2 April 2011, Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai
   - Sri Lanka batted first: 274/6 (Jayawardene 103*)
   - India: 277/4 in 48.2 overs (Gambhir 97, Dhoni 91*)
   - Dhoni hit Nuwan Kulasekara for a six over long-on to win the World Cup
   - That six is one of the most iconic moments in all of cricket history
   - It was India's first WC win in 28 years (since 1983) and on home soil
   - MS Dhoni was Player of the Match in the final

2. SACHIN TENDULKAR FINALLY WINS A WORLD CUP
   - After 6 World Cup campaigns (1992-2011), Sachin finally won the tournament
   - He was carried on the shoulders of his teammates in an emotional victory lap
   - Sachin's quote: "Tendulkar has carried Indian cricket for 22 years. Now it's time we carry him."
   - This was Sachin's last World Cup; he retired from ODIs in 2012

3. KEVIN O'BRIEN'S FASTEST WORLD CUP CENTURY
   - Ireland vs England, Group B, 2 March 2011, M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bangalore
   - England scored 327/8
   - Ireland chased it down in 49.1 overs — one of the greatest chases in WC history
   - Kevin O'Brien scored 113 off 63 balls (13 fours, 6 sixes)
   - His century came off just 50 balls — fastest WC century at that time
   - Ireland won by 3 wickets

4. YUVRAJ SINGH'S ALL-ROUND EXCELLENCE — PLAYER OF THE TOURNAMENT
   - Yuvraj scored 362 runs (avg 90.50) and took 15 wickets (best 5/31)
   - Only player to score 300+ runs and take 15+ wickets in a single WC
   - Named Player of the Tournament
   - Was later diagnosed with a rare germ cell cancer, making his achievement even more remarkable
   - Key innings: 113 vs West Indies, 57* vs Pakistan (SF)
   - Key bowling: 5/31 vs Ireland

5. INDIA vs PAKISTAN SEMI-FINAL — THE MEGA CLASH
   - 30 March 2011, PCA Stadium, Mohali
   - India: 260/9, Pakistan: 231 all out. India won by 29 runs.
   - Sachin scored 85, Wahab Riaz took 5/46
   - Attended by both the Indian PM and Pakistani PM — historic moment of diplomacy
   - Estimated TV viewership of over 1 billion

6. MAHELA JAYAWARDENE'S 103* IN THE FINAL (IN A LOSING CAUSE)
   - Jayawardene scored 103* off 88 balls for Sri Lanka in the 2011 Final
   - One of the finest WC final innings ever, but in a losing cause
   - Sri Lanka scored 274/6 but India chased it down
   - Jayawardene's 103* remains the highest score by a batsman in a WC final in a losing cause

7. GAUTAM GAMBHIR'S UNSUNG 97 IN THE FINAL
   - Gambhir scored 97 off 122 balls to steady India's chase
   - Often overshadowed by Dhoni's finishing six, but Gambhir's innings was the backbone of the chase
   - He was dismissed for 97, missing out on a well-deserved century

8. ZAHEER KHAN AND SHAHID AFRIDI — JOINT LEADING WICKET-TAKERS
   - Both took 21 wickets each in the tournament
   - Zaheer Khan was crucial for India; Shahid Afridi for Pakistan
   - Afridi took 5/16 vs Kenya

9. TILLAKARATNE DILSHAN — LEADING RUN-SCORER
   - 500 runs at average 62.50 — top scorer of the 2011 WC
   - His trademark "Dilscoop" shot was the most talked-about innovation

10. ENGLAND'S EMBARRASSING LOSS TO IRELAND AND BANGLADESH
    - England lost to both Ireland (in the famous O'Brien match) and Bangladesh
    - Despite this, England still advanced to the quarter-final

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
2015 ICC CRICKET WORLD CUP — AUSTRALIA, NEW ZEALAND
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Format: 14 teams, 2 pools (Pool A: 7 teams, Pool B: 7 teams), Quarter-finals, Semi-finals, Final.
Winner: Australia. Runner-up: New Zealand.
Player of the Tournament: Mitchell Starc.

KEY MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

1. AB DE VILLIERS' 31-BALL CENTURY vs WEST INDIES
   - South Africa vs West Indies, Pool B, 27 February 2015, Sydney
   - De Villiers scored 162* off 66 balls (17 fours, 8 sixes)
   - His century came off just 31 balls — FASTEST CENTURY IN WORLD CUP HISTORY
   - South Africa scored 408/5
   - One of the most explosive innings ever witnessed in cricket

2. MARTIN GUPTILL'S 237* vs WEST INDIES — HIGHEST WC SCORE EVER
   - New Zealand vs West Indies, Quarter-Final, 21 March 2015, Wellington
   - Guptill scored 237* off 163 balls (24 fours, 11 sixes)
   - Highest individual score in World Cup history (still stands as of 2023)
   - New Zealand scored 393/6 and won by 143 runs
   - Guptill went from 0 to 237* in a single match

3. CHRIS GAYLE'S 215 vs ZIMBABWE — FIRST DOUBLE CENTURY IN WC
   - West Indies vs Zimbabwe, Pool B, 24 February 2015, Canberra
   - Gayle scored 215 off 147 balls (10 fours, 16 sixes)
   - First double century in World Cup history
   - 16 sixes in a single innings — WC record
   - West Indies scored 372/2

4. BRENDON MCCULLUM'S AGGRESSIVE APPROACH
   - McCullum set the tone for New Zealand with blistering starts
   - Scored 77 off 25 balls vs England (opening game of the tournament)
   - Hit the fastest WC fifty (off 18 balls at that time)
   - New Zealand went unbeaten in the pool stage

5. GRANT ELLIOTT'S SIX TO REACH THE FINAL
   - New Zealand vs South Africa, Semi-Final, 24 March 2015, Eden Park, Auckland
   - One of the greatest semi-finals ever played
   - South Africa needed to win to break their "chokers" tag
   - NZ needed 12 off 7 balls; Grant Elliott hit Dale Steyn for a six to win
   - South Africa's World Cup curse continued — eliminated in another heartbreaking SF
   - AB de Villiers was in tears after the match

6. MITCHELL STARC — PLAYER OF THE TOURNAMENT
   - 22 wickets at average 10.18, economy 3.50 — dominated the tournament
   - Best figures: 6/28 vs New Zealand (pool stage)
   - Left-arm pace was virtually unplayable

7. AUSTRALIA 417/6 vs AFGHANISTAN — HIGHEST TEAM TOTAL
   - Australia scored 417/6 vs Afghanistan at Perth — highest team total in WC history (2003-2023)
   - David Warner scored 178

8. IRELAND BEAT WEST INDIES
   - Pool B, 16 February 2015, Nelson, New Zealand
   - Ireland chased 305 to beat West Indies by 4 wickets
   - Another upset by the Irish at a World Cup

9. TIM SOUTHEE'S 7/33 vs ENGLAND
   - New Zealand vs England, Pool A, 20 February 2015, Wellington
   - Southee took 7/33 to bowl England out for 123
   - England had a terrible tournament (lost to NZ, Australia, SL, Bangladesh)

10. JP DUMINY'S HAT-TRICK vs SRI LANKA
    - South Africa vs Sri Lanka, Quarter-Final, 18 March 2015, Sydney
    - Duminy took a hat-trick to seal the match
    - South Africa won by 9 wickets
    - First hat-trick in a WC knockout match since 2003

11. THE FINAL — AUSTRALIA DEMOLISH NEW ZEALAND
    - Final, 29 March 2015, MCG, Melbourne (attendance: 93,013)
    - New Zealand batted first: 183 all out (Mitchell Starc 2/20, Faulkner 3/36)
    - Australia: 186/3 in 33.1 overs (Clarke 74 in his last ODI)
    - Australia won by 7 wickets; their 5th World Cup title

2019 ICC CRICKET WORLD CUP — ENGLAND AND WALES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Format: 10 teams, round-robin (each team plays 9 matches), Semi-finals, Final.
Winner: England (first-ever WC title). Runner-up: New Zealand.
Player of the Tournament: Kane Williamson.

KEY MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

1. THE GREATEST FINAL IN CRICKET HISTORY — SUPER OVER DRAMA
   - England vs New Zealand, Final, 14 July 2019, Lord's, London
   - New Zealand batted first: 241/8 (50 overs) — Nicholls 55, Latham 47
   - England: 241 all out (50 overs) — Stokes 84*, Buttler 59
   - MATCH TIED AT 241!
   - SUPER OVER: England (Stokes & Buttler) scored 15/0. New Zealand (Neesham & Guptill) scored 15/1
   - SUPER OVER ALSO TIED AT 15!
   - England won the World Cup on BOUNDARY COUNT — England hit more boundaries in the match
   - England: 26 boundaries total (including super over)
   - New Zealand: 17 boundaries total
   - Martin Guptill was run out on the last ball of the Super Over by Jos Buttler
   - Ben Stokes was Player of the Match
   - This is widely considered the greatest cricket match of all time

2. THE OVERTHROW CONTROVERSY — BEN STOKES INCIDENT
   - In the 50th over of England's innings with 9 needed to win
   - Stokes hit the ball towards the boundary, ran back for 2
   - Martin Guptill's throw from deep hit Stokes' bat while he was diving back to make his crease
   - The ball deflected off Stokes' bat and went to the boundary rope
   - Umpires awarded 6 runs (2 completed + 4 for the overthrow)
   - Many argued it should have been 5 runs (not 6) as the batsmen hadn't crossed when the throw was released
   - This incident directly impacted the match result and remains one of the most controversial moments in cricket
   - The ICC later changed the law in 2022 regarding overthrow rules

3. ROHIT SHARMA'S 5 CENTURIES — ALL-TIME WC RECORD
   - Rohit scored 5 centuries in the 2019 WC — most by any batter in a single WC edition
   - Centuries: 122* vs South Africa, 140 vs Pakistan, 104 vs England, 102 vs Bangladesh, 103 vs Sri Lanka
   - Total: 648 runs in 9 matches
   - Set a record that may never be broken

4. JOFRA ARCHER BOWLED THE SUPER OVER
   - England's Jofra Archer, playing in his debut World Cup, bowled the most pressured over in cricket history
   - He held his nerve to defend 15 runs in the Super Over
   - Neesham hit him for a six but Archer came back brilliantly
   - Guptill was run out off the last ball attempting the second run to tie

5. KANE WILLIAMSON — PLAYER OF THE TOURNAMENT
   - 578 runs at average 82.57 — led from the front as captain
   - Scored 148 vs West Indies (key innings)
   - Captained with dignity and grace; praised even in defeat

6. MITCHELL STARC — 27 WICKETS (MOST EVER IN A SINGLE WC)
   - Starc took 27 wickets in 10 matches — all-time record for most wickets in a single WC
   - Despite Australia not reaching the final, Starc's bowling was exceptional

7. PAKISTAN BEAT ENGLAND EARLY
   - Pakistan beat tournament hosts England in the group stage
   - This loss put England's campaign in jeopardy early on
   - England had to win their remaining matches to qualify for semi-finals

8. ENGLAND'S JOURNEY FROM 2015 HUMILIATION TO 2019 GLORY
   - England were eliminated in the group stage of the 2015 WC
   - Completely revamped their ODI approach under Eoin Morgan's captaincy
   - Adopted aggressive batting tactics, changed their squad composition
   - Won the 2019 WC — one of the greatest transformations in cricket history

9. INDIA vs PAKISTAN — ROHIT SHARMA DOMINATES
   - India beat Pakistan at Old Trafford
   - Rohit Sharma scored 140 — dominated the Pakistan bowling
   - Kuldeep Yadav bowled brilliantly

10. NEW ZEALAND'S RAIN-AFFECTED SEMI-FINAL vs INDIA
    - Semi-Final at Old Trafford, played over 2 days due to rain (July 9-10, 2019)
    - New Zealand: 239/8 (first day), India: 221/3 → 24/4 → eventually 221 all out
    - NZ won by 18 runs
    - Matt Henry and Trent Boult bowled superbly
    - Jadeja's 77 nearly pulled off a miraculous chase for India
    - MS Dhoni run out for 50 (Guptill's direct hit) — his last international innings

11. DRS CONTROVERSY IN THE 2019 FINAL
    - During the final, there were several DRS reviews that were widely discussed
    - Key moment: The question of whether the overthrow should have been 5 or 6 runs was not a DRS issue
    - The umpiring decisions were generally sound, but the overthrow ruling by on-field umpire Kumar Dharmasena was the most debated
    - Dharmasena later admitted he may have made an error in awarding 6 runs for the overthrow

12. BOUNDARY COUNT RULE ABOLISHED
    - After the 2019 final, the ICC faced enormous criticism for the boundary count rule
    - The rule was later replaced: from 2019 onwards, if a Super Over is tied, additional Super Overs are played
    - The boundary count rule will never be used again
    - New Zealand were widely seen as unlucky to lose the World Cup on a rule that many considered arbitrary

2023 ICC CRICKET WORLD CUP — INDIA
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Format: 10 teams, round-robin (each team plays 9 matches), Semi-finals, Final.
Winner: Australia (6th WC title). Runner-up: India.
Player of the Tournament: Virat Kohli.

KEY MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

1. INDIA'S DOMINANT LEAGUE STAGE — 10 WINS IN 10 MATCHES
   - India won all 10 league matches — the first team to do so in a 10-team round-robin WC
   - Dominant from start to finish in the group stage
   - But lost the match that mattered most — the final

2. AUSTRALIA BEAT INDIA IN THE FINAL — STUNNING UPSET
   - Final, 19 November 2023, Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad (130,000 fans)
   - India batted first: 240 all out (Kohli 54, KL Rahul 66)
   - Australia: 241/4 in 43 overs (Travis Head 137, Labuschagne 58*)
   - Australia won by 6 wickets
   - Travis Head was Player of the Match — his 137 demolished India's bowling
   - India were heavy favorites; the packed stadium went silent as Australia chased it down

3. VIRAT KOHLI EQUALS SACHIN'S RECORD OF 49 ODI CENTURIES
   - During the 2023 WC, Kohli scored his 49th ODI century to equal Sachin Tendulkar's record
   - That century came against South Africa in the league stage
   - Kohli was Player of the Tournament with 765 runs — most in a single WC edition

4. GLENN MAXWELL'S 201* vs AFGHANISTAN — THE GREATEST ODI INNINGS?
   - Australia vs Afghanistan, Group Stage, 7 November 2023, Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai
   - Afghanistan scored 291
   - Australia collapsed to 91/7 — looked completely out of the game
   - Glenn Maxwell, battling severe cramps and a bad back, scored 201* off 128 balls
   - He couldn't run properly in the final overs, hitting boundaries from where he stood
   - Australia won by 3 wickets
   - 201* is the highest score in a WC chase and the 2nd highest in any WC innings
   - Widely regarded as one of the greatest ODI innings in the history of cricket
   - The only double century scored while chasing in WC history

5. MOHAMMED SHAMI'S 7/57 vs NEW ZEALAND — SEMI-FINAL DOMINANCE
   - India vs New Zealand, Semi-Final, 15 November 2023, Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai
   - Shami took 7/57 — best bowling figures in a WC semi-final ever
   - NZ were bowled out for 327; India chased it down with Kohli's 117
   - Shami took 24 wickets in just 7 matches in the tournament

6. TRAVIS HEAD'S 137 IN THE FINAL
   - Head walked in at 47/2 in the final and played the innings of his life
   - Scored 137 off 120 balls (15 fours, 4 sixes)
   - Highest score in a WC final while chasing
   - His partnership with Labuschagne (192 runs for the 3rd wicket) sealed the match

7. INDIA'S HEARTBREAK — THE UNBEATEN RUN ENDS
   - India had won 10 consecutive matches heading into the final
   - The weight of 1.4 billion people's expectations was enormous
   - India's batting lineup, which had been outstanding all tournament, underperformed in the final
   - Kohli (54) and Rahul (66) scored but no one made a big hundred
   - The dream of winning a WC at home was shattered

8. ROHIT SHARMA'S AGGRESSIVE CAPTAINCY
   - Rohit batted with a strike rate of 125+ throughout the tournament
   - Changed India's approach to white-ball batting — attack from ball one
   - Scored 597 runs in 11 matches

9. SOUTH AFRICA'S CLOSE SEMI-FINAL LOSS TO AUSTRALIA
   - Semi-Final, 16 November 2023, Eden Gardens, Kolkata
   - South Africa had a strong tournament but lost in the semi-final to Australia
   - Continuing South Africa's unfortunate streak of never reaching a WC final

10. NETHERLANDS' SHOCK WIN OVER SOUTH AFRICA
    - Group Stage: Netherlands beat South Africa at Dharamsala
    - One of the upsets of the tournament

11. AFGHANISTAN'S COMPETITIVE SHOWING
    - Afghanistan won matches against England, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka
    - Rashid Khan and Rahmanullah Gurbaz were outstanding
    - Their most memorable match was the Maxwell 201* game (where they were on the losing side)

12. QUINTON DE KOCK'S 174 vs BANGLADESH
    - De Kock scored 174 vs Bangladesh — 2nd highest score of the tournament after Maxwell's 201*
    - South Africa posted 382/5

13. ENGLAND'S TITLE DEFENSE FAILURE
    - Defending champions England had a terrible tournament
    - Lost to Afghanistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, and others
    - Failed to qualify for semi-finals
    - Worst title defense in WC history
