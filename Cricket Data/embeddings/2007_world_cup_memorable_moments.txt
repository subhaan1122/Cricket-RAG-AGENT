ICC Cricket World Cup 2007 — Memorable Moments, Records & Key Events
======================================================================

Tournament: 2007 ICC Cricket World Cup
Host Country: West Indies (various Caribbean islands)
Dates: 13 March – 28 April 2007
Teams: 16 (Australia, Bangladesh, Bermuda, Canada, England, India, Ireland, Kenya, Netherlands, New Zealand, Pakistan, Scotland, South Africa, Sri Lanka, West Indies, Zimbabwe)
Winner: Australia (defeated Sri Lanka in the Final)
Runner-up: Sri Lanka
Player of the Tournament: Glenn McGrath
Top Run Scorer: Matthew Hayden (659 runs)
Top Wicket Taker: Glenn McGrath (26 wickets)

=== TOP MEMORABLE MOMENTS ===

1. The Biggest Upset in World Cup History — Ireland Beat Pakistan:
   Date: 17 March 2007. Venue: Sabina Park, Kingston, Jamaica.
   Ireland beat Pakistan by 3 wickets in one of the greatest upsets in cricket history.
   Pakistan batted first and scored 132 all out. Boyd Rankin took 3/32.
   Ireland chased it down reaching 133/7 with Niall O'Brien scoring 72.
   This result, combined with Pakistan's loss to West Indies, eliminated Pakistan from the group stage.
   This was the match after which Pakistan coach Bob Woolmer was found dead in his hotel room on 18 March 2007 — one of the most shocking and tragic events in cricket history.

2. Bob Woolmer's Death:
   Pakistan coach Bob Woolmer was found unconscious in his hotel room in Kingston, Jamaica on 18 March 2007, the day after Pakistan's elimination from the World Cup.
   He was pronounced dead at the hospital. Initial reports suggested he was murdered (manual strangulation).
   A Jamaican police investigation followed, but in June 2007, the verdict was changed to death by natural causes (heart failure).
   This remains one of the most tragic and controversial events in World Cup history.

3. Bangladesh Beat India in the Group Stage:
   Date: 17 March 2007. Venue: Queen's Park Oval, Port of Spain.
   Bangladesh beat India by 5 wickets in a massive upset.
   India scored 191 all out. Mashrafe Mortaza took 4/38.
   Bangladesh chased it down with 5 wickets in hand, with Mushfiqur Rahim and Tamim Iqbal contributing.
   Combined with India's loss to Sri Lanka, India were eliminated in the group stage — a humiliating exit for one of cricket's biggest teams.
   This was India's earliest exit from a World Cup since 1999.

4. India's Group Stage Elimination:
   India failed to advance past the group stage, losing to Bangladesh and Sri Lanka.
   Captain Rahul Dravid faced massive criticism. The team returned to effigy-burning protests across India.
   This World Cup exit led to major changes in Indian cricket management and coaching.

5. Australia's Third Consecutive World Cup Title:
   Australia won the 2007 World Cup, their 3rd consecutive title (1999, 2003, 2007) and 4th overall.
   They went unbeaten again, winning all 11 matches.
   This extended their unbeaten World Cup streak to 29 consecutive wins (2003 full tournament + 2007 full tournament).
   Adam Gilchrist's stunning century in the final sealed their dominance.

6. The 2007 Final — Australia 281/4 vs Sri Lanka 215/8:
   Date: 28 April 2007. Venue: Kensington Oval, Bridgetown, Barbados.
   The final started under lights but was interrupted by rain and bad light.
   Adam Gilchrist scored 149 off 104 balls — one of the greatest World Cup final innings ever.
   He used a squash ball inside his left glove for better grip — an unusual technique revelation.
   Australia posted 281/4. Sri Lanka were restricted to 215/8 (36 overs, rain-revised target 269 in 36 overs).
   Glenn McGrath, in his final international match, took 0/14 in 4 overs and was named Player of the Tournament.
   Player of the Match: Adam Gilchrist.
   Turning Point: Gilchrist's explosive batting destroyed Sri Lanka's bowling attack in the first 20 overs.

7. Glenn McGrath's Farewell:
   The 2007 World Cup final was Glenn McGrath's last international match.
   He finished with 26 wickets in the tournament — the top wicket-taker.
   He won the Player of the Tournament award — a fitting farewell to one of the greatest fast bowlers ever.
   Across World Cups 2003 and 2007, McGrath was virtually unplayable.

8. The Super Eight Controversy:
   The 2007 format used a Super Eight stage where 8 teams played each other.
   Points from the group stage were carried forward — only points from matches against other Super Eight qualifiers.
   This format was criticized for being too long and making early upsets less meaningful.

9. Herschelle Gibbs's Six Sixes:
   Herschelle Gibbs of South Africa hit six sixes in one over bowled by Daan van Bunge of the Netherlands.
   Date: 16 March 2007. Venue: Warner Park, Basseterre, St Kitts.
   Gibbs became only the third player in international cricket to achieve this feat.
   South Africa scored 353/3, with Gibbs making 72 off 40 balls.

10. Bermuda's World Cup Appearance:
    Bermuda qualified for their only World Cup in 2007.
    They lost all 3 group matches but the experience was historic for the tiny island nation.
    Their match against India featured some viral moments including their larger-bodied players.

=== RECORDS SET IN 2007 WORLD CUP ===

- Adam Gilchrist 149 off 104 balls in the World Cup final — highest score in a WC final
- Australia's 3rd consecutive World Cup title (1999, 2003, 2007)
- Herschelle Gibbs — 6 sixes in an over vs Netherlands
- Glenn McGrath — 26 wickets, Player of the Tournament (his farewell tournament)
- Matthew Hayden — 659 runs (tournament top scorer)
- Ireland beating Pakistan — one of the biggest upsets in WC history
- Australia unbeaten run extended to 29 consecutive WC wins

=== MAJOR UPSETS ===

1. Ireland beat Pakistan by 3 wickets (Group D, 17 March 2007) — led to Pakistan's elimination
2. Bangladesh beat India by 5 wickets (Group B, 17 March 2007) — contributed to India's elimination
3. India eliminated in group stage for the first time since 1999
4. Pakistan eliminated in group stage after losing to Ireland and West Indies

=== TOURNAMENT FORMAT 2007 ===

Group Stage: 16 teams divided into 4 groups of 4. Each team played every other team in their group once. Top 2 from each group advanced to the Super Eight.
Super Eight: 8 teams played each of the teams from other groups (carried forward results against other qualifiers from own group). Top 4 advanced to semi-finals.
Semi-Finals: Two semi-finals.
Final: Kensington Oval, Bridgetown, Barbados on 28 April 2007.
Points: 2 for a win, 1 for tie/no-result, 0 for a loss.
Points Carry Forward Rule: In the Super Eight stage, teams carried forward the points earned in group matches ONLY against teams that also qualified for the Super Eight from the same group.

=== KEY UNUSUAL EVENTS ===

- Bob Woolmer's death in Jamaica overshadowed the tournament
- Rain disrupted the final, requiring a revised DLS target
- The tournament lasted 47 days — the longest World Cup ever, drawing criticism
- Fans in India burned effigies of players after the team's early exit
- Australia were so dominant that the tournament lacked competitive tension in knockout stages
