ICC Cricket World Cup 2015 — Memorable Moments, Records & Key Events
======================================================================

Tournament: 2015 ICC Cricket World Cup
Host Countries: Australia, New Zealand
Dates: 14 February – 29 March 2015
Teams: 14 (Afghanistan, Australia, Bangladesh, England, India, Ireland, New Zealand, Pakistan, Scotland, South Africa, Sri Lanka, United Arab Emirates, West Indies, Zimbabwe)
Winner: Australia (defeated New Zealand in the Final)
Runner-up: New Zealand
Player of the Tournament: Mitchell Starc
Top Run Scorer: Martin Guptill (547 runs)
Top Wicket Taker: Mitchell Starc (22 wickets, average 10.18, economy 3.50)

=== TOP MEMORABLE MOMENTS ===

1. The Greatest World Cup Knock? — Martin Guptill's 237* vs West Indies:
   Date: 21 March 2015. Venue: Wellington Regional Stadium, Wellington. Quarter-Final.
   Martin Guptill scored 237* off 163 balls — the highest individual score in World Cup history.
   He hit 24 fours and 11 sixes. New Zealand posted 393/6.
   West Indies were bowled out for 250. New Zealand won by 143 runs.
   This surpassed Gary Kirsten's 188* (1996) as the highest individual World Cup score.
   Guptill's innings remains THE highest score in any World Cup match to this day (as of 2023).

2. The 2015 Final — Australia 186/3 vs New Zealand 183 all out:
   Date: 29 March 2015. Venue: Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG), Melbourne.
   New Zealand batted first and were bowled out for 183 in 45 overs.
   Grant Elliott (83 off 82 balls) was the top scorer for New Zealand.
   Mitchell Starc took 2/20 in 8 overs with the new ball — removing both openers cheaply.
   Australia chased it down comfortably, reaching 186/3 in 33.1 overs.
   Steve Smith scored 56* off 71 balls to guide Australia home.
   Player of the Match: James Faulkner (3/36).
   Australia won by 7 wickets, claiming their 5th World Cup title.
   Turning Point: Starc's opening spell that removed Brendon McCullum (0) and Williamson (12).

3. Mitchell Starc's Tournament Dominance:
   Mitchell Starc was named Player of the Tournament for his extraordinary bowling.
   22 wickets in 8 matches at an average of 10.18 and economy of 3.50.
   His ability to swing the ball at 150+ km/h made him virtually unplayable.
   His yorker to dismiss Brendon McCullum in the final was a tournament-defining delivery.

4. AB de Villiers' 162 off 66 balls vs West Indies:
   Date: 27 February 2015. Venue: Sydney Cricket Ground, Sydney.
   AB de Villiers smashed 162 off just 66 balls — the fastest 150 in ODI history at that time.
   He hit 17 fours and 8 sixes. South Africa posted 408/5.
   This was the highest individual score by a South African in World Cup history.
   South Africa won by 257 runs — one of the largest margins in WC history.

5. South Africa's Semi-Final Heartbreak (Again) — Grant Elliott's Six:
   Date: 24 March 2015. Venue: Eden Park, Auckland.
   South Africa vs New Zealand semi-final — one of the greatest World Cup matches ever.
   South Africa posted 281/5. Dale Steyn and Morne Morkel gave everything with the ball.
   New Zealand needed 298 (DLS revised) and were struggling at 149/4.
   Grant Elliott hit a six off Dale Steyn's penultimate over to win the match for New Zealand.
   Elliott's match-winning 84* off 73 balls ended South Africa's World Cup dream once more.
   Dale Steyn was in tears. The image of Elliott consoling Steyn became one of the most poignant World Cup photographs.
   South Africa's "knockout chokers" narrative reached its most painful chapter.

6. Brendon McCullum's Explosive Batting:
   New Zealand captain Brendon McCullum was the tournament's most exciting batsman.
   He scored a century off just 77 balls vs England — setting the tone for NZ's campaign.
   McCullum's ultra-aggressive approach, especially in the powerplay, changed how teams thought about ODI batting.

7. Ireland Nearly Stun Australia:
   In a group match at WACA Perth, Ireland came agonizingly close to beating Australia.
   Ireland scored 304. Australia chased it down in the final over, winning by 2 wickets.

8. England's Dismal Performance:
   England had a terrible tournament, losing to Bangladesh, New Zealand, Sri Lanka, and Australia.
   They were eliminated in the group stage — prompting a complete overhaul of their white-ball cricket philosophy.
   This humiliation led to the appointment of Trevor Bayliss as coach and the aggressive approach that eventually won them the 2019 World Cup.

9. Afghanistan's First World Cup:
   Afghanistan played their first ever ODI World Cup match.
   They scored a historic win vs Scotland by 1 wicket in a thrilling match — their first-ever World Cup victory.
   Shapoor Zadran's winning shot celebrations went viral.

10. The Tournament of High Scores:
    The 2015 World Cup produced the most 300+ team totals in any World Cup.
    Flat pitches in Australia, combined with aggressive batting approaches, led to record-breaking scores.
    Chris Gayle scored 215 vs Zimbabwe — the first-ever double century in World Cup history.
    Gayle hit 10 fours and 16 sixes in his 215 off 147 balls. This was the highest score in 2015 WC before Guptill's 237*.

- Chris Gayle 215 vs Zimbabwe — first double century in World Cup history

- Mitchell Starc — 22 wickets at average 10.18, Player of Tournament
- South Africa 408/5 vs West Indies — one of the highest team totals in WC
- Most 300+ team totals in any single World Cup tournament
- Australia's 5th World Cup title

4. Sri Lanka beat England by 9 wickets (Group A)

- Chris Gayle scored the first-ever World Cup double century (215 vs Zimbabwe)
- This was the last World Cup to feature quarter-finals — the format was changed for 2019
- England's dismal performance led to a complete white-ball revolution
