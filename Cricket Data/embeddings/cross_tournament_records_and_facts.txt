=== WORLD CUP FORMAT EVOLUTION ===

1975-1979: 8 teams. Round-robin groups, semi-finals, final. 60 overs per side.
1983: 8 teams. Round-robin groups (played twice), semi-finals, final. 60 overs.
1987: 8 teams. First WC outside England. Reduced to 50 overs per side.
1992: 9 teams. Round-robin (every team plays every other), semi-finals, final. Colored clothing, white ball, day-night matches introduced.
1996: 12 teams. Groups + quarter-finals + semi-finals + final.
1999: 12 teams. Groups + Super Six + semi-finals + final.
2003: 14 teams. Groups + Super Six + semi-finals + final.
2007: 16 teams. Groups + Super Eight + semi-finals + final.
2011: 14 teams. Groups + quarter-finals + semi-finals + final.
2015: 14 teams. Pools + quarter-finals + semi-finals + final. LAST WC with quarter-finals.
2019: 10 teams. Round-robin (every team plays every other) + semi-finals + final. NO quarter-finals.
2023: 10 teams. Same round-robin format as 2019.

=== SUPER OVERS IN WORLD CUP ===

There has been ONLY ONE Super Over in World Cup history — the 2019 Final.
England vs New Zealand: Match tied at 241. Super Over tied at 15-15. England won via boundary countback.
The boundary countback rule was abolished after the 2019 World Cup.

=== RICKY PONTING'S WORLD CUP RECORD ===

Ponting played 5 World Cups (1996, 1999, 2003, 2007, 2011).
World Cup runs: 1,743 in 46 matches.
Centuries: He scored 140* in the 2003 final vs India (Player of the Match).
In the 2007 final vs Sri Lanka, Ponting captained Australia to victory. He scored 37 in that final (not a big score, but Australia won comfortably).
He was captain for Australia's 3 consecutive WC wins (2003, 2007).
In 2011, Ponting's last WC, Australia were eliminated in the quarter-final by India.

=== VIRAT KOHLI'S WORLD CUP RECORD ===

Kohli played 4 World Cups (2011, 2015, 2019, 2023).
2011: Scored 282 runs (avg 35.25), including 100* vs Bangladesh. WON the World Cup.
2015: Scored 305 runs (avg 50.83), 1 century. India lost in semi-final to Australia.
2019: Scored 443 runs (avg 55.37), India lost in semi-final to New Zealand.
2023: Scored 765 runs (avg 95.62), 3 centuries. India lost in final to Australia. Player of Tournament.

Kohli's World Cup knockout record (semi-finals and finals only):
2011 final: Scored 35 (India won)
2015 semi vs Australia: Scored 1 (India lost)
2019 semi vs New Zealand: Scored 1 (India lost)
2023 final vs Australia: Scored 54 (India lost)

=== AB DE VILLIERS' WORLD CUP RECORD ===

AB de Villiers played 3 World Cups (2007, 2011, 2015).
2007: South Africa eliminated in Super Eights. De Villiers scored some runs but was not dominant.
2011: South Africa eliminated in quarter-final by New Zealand. De Villiers scored 353 runs.
2015: De Villiers' best WC. He scored 482 runs at avg 96.40, including 162* off 66 balls vs WI.
South Africa lost the semi-final to New Zealand (Grant Elliott's six).
De Villiers NEVER scored the same score in all 3 World Cups. His scores varied match to match.

=== CHRIS GAYLE'S WORLD CUP SIXES ===

Chris Gayle played 5 World Cups (2003, 2007, 2011, 2015, 2019).
He is one of the biggest six-hitters in World Cup history.
His 215 vs Zimbabwe in 2015 included 16 sixes — the most in a single WC innings.
Career WC sixes: Gayle hit a significant number across his 5 tournaments.
His most prolific WC was 2015 where he scored 340 runs with many sixes.

=== SACHIN TENDULKAR'S WORLD CUP RECORD ===

Tendulkar played 6 World Cups (1992, 1996, 1999, 2003, 2007, 2011).
Total: 2,278 runs in 45 matches at average 56.95 — MOST runs in WC history.
Centuries: 6 centuries in World Cups.
Best WC: 2003 — 673 runs including 98 vs Pakistan (iconic innings at Centurion).
2011: Tendulkar's last WC. India won. He scored 482 runs. Carried off the field by teammates.
Tendulkar holds the record for most 50+ scores in World Cups.

=== MS DHONI'S WORLD CUP RECORD ===

Dhoni played 4 World Cups (2007, 2011, 2015, 2019).
2007: Young Dhoni was part of the squad but India was eliminated in group stage.
2011: Captain. Won the World Cup. Hit the winning six (a massive six over long-on off Nuwan Kulasekara). Scored 91* in the final.
2015: Captain. India lost in semi-final to Australia. Dhoni scored 65 in that semi.
2019: India lost in semi-final to New Zealand. Dhoni scored 50 before being run out by Guptill's direct hit. This was his LAST international innings. He retired from international cricket on 15 August 2020.
