ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 — Memorable Moments, Records & Key Events
======================================================================

Tournament: 2023 ICC Cricket World Cup
Host Country: India
Dates: 5 October – 19 November 2023
Teams: 10 (Afghanistan, Australia, Bangladesh, England, India, Netherlands, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka)
Winner: Australia (defeated India in the Final)
Runner-up: India
Player of the Tournament: Virat Kohli (India)
Top Run Scorer: Virat Kohli (765 runs, 3 centuries)
Top Wicket Taker: Mohammed Shami (24 wickets in just 7 matches)

=== THE 2023 WORLD CUP FINAL ===

Date: 19 November 2023
Venue: Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad, India (capacity: 132,000)

FIRST INNINGS — India batted first:
India scored 240 all out in 50 overs — a disappointing total given their dominance all tournament.
Virat Kohli scored 54 (his 50th ODI century came earlier in the tournament, not in the final).
KL Rahul scored 66 off 107 balls.
Pat Cummins took 2/34 and Mitchell Starc took 3/55.
Australia's bowling was disciplined — they restricted India on a surface that offered some assistance.

SECOND INNINGS — Australia's chase:
Travis Head scored a stunning 137 off 120 balls — one of the greatest World Cup final knocks.
Head was dropped by KL Rahul early in his innings — a costly miss that changed the match.
Marnus Labuschagne scored 58 off 110 balls, providing a crucial partnership with Head.
Australia reached 241/4 in 43 overs — winning by 6 wickets.
Player of the Match: Travis Head.

Turning Point: KL Rahul's dropped catch of Travis Head when Head was on 6. Head went on to score 137.
This was India's FIRST home World Cup final loss and ended their 10-match winning streak in the tournament.
Over 130,000 fans in the stadium fell silent as Australia celebrated.

Australia won their RECORD 6th World Cup title (1987, 1999, 2003, 2007, 2015, 2023).
They are the most successful team in World Cup history.

=== TOP MEMORABLE MOMENTS ===

1. India's Perfect 10 — 10 Consecutive Wins Then Final Loss:
   India won ALL 10 matches before the final — a record unbeaten streak in a single World Cup.
   They dominated every opponent in the group stage and semi-final.
   But they lost the one match that mattered most — the final.
   This was heartbreaking for 1.4 billion Indian fans and the 130,000+ in attendance.

2. Virat Kohli's Record 50th ODI Century:
   Date: 15 November 2023. Match: India vs South Africa, Semi-Final, Kolkata.
   Kohli scored 101* to guide India to the final.
   This was his 50th ODI century — breaking Sachin Tendulkar's all-time record of 49 ODI centuries.
   Tendulkar himself was present at Eden Gardens to witness the moment.
   One of the most emotional moments in cricket history — Kohli touched Tendulkar's feet in the stands.
   Kohli finished the tournament with 765 runs — the most by any batter in 2023 WC.

3. Mohammed Shami's Extraordinary Campaign:
   Shami took 24 wickets in just 7 matches at an average of 10.70.
   He was not in India's original XI — he came in as a replacement after the first match.
   Shami took 7/57 vs New Zealand in the semi-final — the best figures by an Indian in World Cups.
   His 5-wicket hauls: 5/54 vs Sri Lanka, 5/18 vs England (yes, FIVE for EIGHTEEN).
   He had the best strike rate (10.7) and average (10.70) of any bowler in the tournament.

4. Travis Head's Final Masterclass — 137 off 120:
   Head's century in the final was arguably the greatest pressure knock in recent WC history.
   He was dropped on 6 by KL Rahul — and made India pay the ultimate price.
   His 137 included 15 fours and 4 sixes.
   Head had been in excellent form but this innings was on another level.

5. Afghanistan's Historic Semi-Final Dream:
   Afghanistan had their best-ever World Cup, beating England, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka.
   They came close to qualifying for the semi-finals for the first time.
   Rahmanullah Gurbaz (568 runs) and Ibrahim Zadran were outstanding.
   Their victory over defending champions England (69-run win) was historic.

6. England's Title Defense Disaster:
   Defending champions England had one of the worst title defenses ever.
   They lost to New Zealand, Afghanistan, India, South Africa, Sri Lanka, and Australia.
   They won only 3 of 9 group matches and were eliminated in the group stage.
   Jos Buttler's captaincy was heavily criticized.

7. Netherlands' Heroics:
   Netherlands, the only Associate team, punched above their weight.
   They beat South Africa by 38 runs — one of the biggest upsets of the tournament.

8. South Africa's Semi-Final Heartbreak (Again):
   South Africa qualified for the semi-final but lost to Australia by 3 wickets.
   Travis Head (52) and David Warner were the key contributors.
   South Africa's "chokers" label in knockout matches continued.

9. Jasprit Bumrah's Return:
   Bumrah returned from a back injury and took 20 wickets at average 18.65.
   His economy of 4.06 was exceptional on Indian pitches.
   He and Shami formed the most lethal pace pair in the tournament.

10. The Greatest ODI Team... That Lost the Final:
    India 2023 is statistically one of the greatest ODI teams ever assembled.
    They scored the most runs, took the most wickets, had the best NRR.
    Rohit Sharma's aggressive captaincy — including hitting 31 sixes in the tournament (personal record).
    Shubman Gill, Shreyas Iyer, and KL Rahul all contributed heavily.
    But they lost the one match that defined everything — the final.

=== TOURNAMENT FORMAT 2023 ===

This World Cup used the SAME round-robin format as 2019:
- 10 teams, every team plays every other team once — 45 group matches.
- Top 4 qualify for semi-finals directly.
- NO quarter-finals.
- Semi-Final 1 (1st vs 4th) and Semi-Final 2 (2nd vs 3rd).
- Points: 2 for win, 1 for tie/NR, 0 for loss.

=== KEY UNUSUAL EVENTS ===

- The Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad (132,000 capacity) hosted the final — the largest cricket ground in the world
- India played ALL their group matches and semi-final at home — leading to debate about home advantage
- Dew factor played a huge role in evening matches — teams batting second had an advantage in many games
- This was potentially Virat Kohli's last ODI World Cup (aged 35 in 2023)
- The tournament saw a return of genuine fast bowling dominance — Starc, Bumrah, Shami, Shaheen, Boult
- Australia have now won the World Cup more times (6) than any other team
- Pat Cummins captained Australia to the World Cup title while also being their frontline fast bowler
