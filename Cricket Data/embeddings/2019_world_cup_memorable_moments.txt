ICC Cricket World Cup 2019 — Memorable Moments, Records & Key Events
======================================================================

Tournament: 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup
Host Country: England & Wales
Dates: 30 May – 14 July 2019
Teams: 10 (Afghanistan, Australia, Bangladesh, England, India, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, West Indies)
Winner: England (defeated New Zealand in the Final via Super Over and boundary countback)
Runner-up: New Zealand
Player of the Tournament: Kane Williamson (New Zealand)
Top Run Scorer: Rohit Sharma (648 runs, 5 centuries)
Top Wicket Taker: Mitchell Starc (27 wickets)

=== THE 2019 WORLD CUP FINAL — THE GREATEST CRICKET MATCH EVER PLAYED ===

Date: 14 July 2019
Venue: Lord's Cricket Ground, London, England

THIS IS THE MOST DRAMATIC CRICKET MATCH IN HISTORY. Here is exactly what happened:

FIRST INNINGS — New Zealand batted first:
New Zealand scored 241/8 in 50 overs.
Henry Nicholls top-scored with 55 off 77 balls.
Tom Latham scored 47.
New Zealand struggled against Chris Woakes (3/37) and Liam Plunkett (3/42).
241 seemed like a below-par total on a decent Lord's pitch.

SECOND INNINGS — England's chase:
England were overwhelming favorites but struggled badly.
England were 86/4 at one stage — in deep trouble.
Ben Stokes (84* off 98 balls) and Jos Buttler (59 off 60 balls) rebuilt the innings.
With 2 balls remaining, England needed 2 runs to win.
A throw from the outfield deflected off Ben Stokes' bat while he was running and went to the boundary — England were awarded 4 overthrows (6 runs total for that delivery).
This was a hugely controversial moment — some believed only 5 runs should have been awarded.
England finished their 50 overs on exactly 241 — SCORES WERE TIED.

THE SUPER OVER:
Because the match was tied after 50 overs each, a Super Over was required.
This was the FIRST EVER Super Over in a World Cup final.

England batted first in the Super Over:
Ben Stokes and Jos Buttler faced Trent Boult.
England scored 15 runs off 6 balls.

New Zealand batted second in the Super Over:
Martin Guptill and Jimmy Neesham faced Jofra Archer.
Jimmy Neesham hit a massive six off the second ball — New Zealand needed 2 off the last ball.
Martin Guptill hit the last ball toward mid-wicket and ran. Jason Roy's throw came to Jos Buttler.
Guptill was RUN OUT attempting the second run. New Zealand scored 15 off their Super Over.

THE SUPER OVER WAS ALSO TIED — 15 runs each.

BOUNDARY COUNTBACK RULE:
Because the Super Over was also tied, the winner was decided by the BOUNDARY COUNTBACK RULE.
This rule stated: "In the event of a tied Super Over, the team that hit more boundaries in the match (including the Super Over) wins."

England hit 26 boundaries in the match (including Super Over): 22 fours + 2 sixes in regulation, plus 2 boundaries in Super Over.
New Zealand hit 17 boundaries in the match (including Super Over): 14 fours + 2 sixes in regulation, plus 1 six in Super Over.

ENGLAND WON THE 2019 WORLD CUP BY BOUNDARY COUNTBACK.
England won their FIRST EVER Cricket World Cup.

This boundary countback rule was widely criticized as unfair. The ICC later ABOLISHED the boundary countback rule.
From 2019 onwards, if a Super Over is tied, another Super Over would be played until there is a winner.

Player of the Match: Ben Stokes.

KEY CONTROVERSY: The overthrow incident — Stokes' bat deflection:
With England needing 9 off 3 balls, Stokes hit the ball and ran. The fielder's throw hit Stokes' bat as he dived to make his ground, and the ball deflected to the boundary.
The umpires awarded 6 runs (the 2 already run + 4 for the overthrow boundary).
Under Law 19.8, some experts argued only 5 runs should have been given (1 run completed + 4 for the overthrow), as the batsmen had not crossed at the moment the throw was made.
This remains one of the most debated umpiring decisions in cricket history.

=== TOP MEMORABLE MOMENTS (Beyond the Final) ===

1. Rohit Sharma's Record 5 Centuries:
   Rohit Sharma scored FIVE centuries in a single World Cup — a record that still stands.
   His centuries: 122* vs South Africa, 140 vs Pakistan, 104 vs Bangladesh, 102 vs England, 103 vs Sri Lanka.
   Total runs: 648 runs in 9 innings at average 81.00.
   He broke Kumar Sangakkara's record of 4 centuries in a single World Cup (set in 2015).

2. David Warner's Resurgence:
   David Warner returned from a 12-month ban for ball-tampering.
   He scored 647 runs — second only to Rohit Sharma.
   His 166 vs Bangladesh was the highest individual score of the tournament.

3. Shakib Al Hasan's All-Round Masterclass:
   Shakib scored 606 runs and took 11 wickets — the best all-round performance in World Cup history.
   He was Bangladesh's best player by a massive margin.

4. Carlos Brathwaite's Near-Miracle vs New Zealand:
   West Indies needed 6 off the last ball vs New Zealand.
   Carlos Brathwaite hit a massive six... but Trent Boult caught him on the boundary!
   Boult's foot touched the boundary rope — but he threw the ball up before stepping back in to complete the catch.
   West Indies lost by 5 runs. One of the most dramatic finishes in WC history.

5. Pakistan's Roller-Coaster Campaign:
   Pakistan lost their first match to West Indies, then beat England (the hosts).
   They remained in contention until the final group matches but were eliminated due to net run rate.
   Babar Azam scored 474 runs — his breakthrough tournament.

6. Mitchell Starc's Record 27 Wickets:
   Mitchell Starc broke Glenn McGrath's all-time World Cup record of 26 wickets in a single tournament.
   Starc took 27 wickets in 10 matches at average 18.59 — the most by any bowler in a single World Cup.
   Glenn McGrath's record (26 in 2007) had stood for 12 years.

7. India's Dominant Campaign Ends in Semi-Final:
   India won 7 of 9 group matches and finished top of the table.
   In the semi-final vs New Zealand (spread over 2 days due to rain), India lost by 18 runs.
   Jadeja (77) and Dhoni (50) nearly pulled off a miracle chase.
   MS Dhoni was run out by Martin Guptill's direct hit — one of the most emotional moments.
   This was Dhoni's last international innings. He never played for India again.

8. Jason Roy and Jonny Bairstow — England's Opening Dominance:
   Roy (443 runs) and Bairstow (532 runs) were the most destructive opening pair.
   They set the platform for England's aggressive approach throughout the tournament.

9. Afghanistan's Rashid Khan Struggle:
   In one of the most difficult days for any bowler, Rashid Khan conceded 110 runs off 9 overs vs England.
   These are the most expensive bowling figures in World Cup history.

10. South Africa's Disappointing Campaign:
    South Africa had a terrible tournament, winning only 3 of 9 matches.
    They were eliminated in the group stage for the first time since 2003.

=== RECORDS SET IN 2019 WORLD CUP ===

- Rohit Sharma: 5 centuries in a single World Cup (record)
- Mitchell Starc: 27 wickets in a single World Cup (record, breaking McGrath's 26)
- First Super Over in a World Cup final
- First World Cup final decided by boundary countback
- Boundary countback rule subsequently abolished by ICC
- Shakib Al Hasan: 606 runs + 11 wickets (best all-round WC performance)
- Rashid Khan: 0/110 off 9 overs vs England (most expensive WC bowling figures)
- England won their first-ever World Cup
- David Warner: 647 runs (second highest in a single WC)

3. West Indies beat Pakistan by 7 wickets (31 May 2019, opening match upset)

=== TOURNAMENT FORMAT 2019 ===

This World Cup used a ROUND-ROBIN format — completely different from previous World Cups.
- Only 10 teams participated (reduced from 14 in 2015).
- Every team played every other team ONCE — 45 group matches total.
- The top 4 teams in the standings qualified directly for the semi-finals.
- There were NO quarter-finals.
- Semi-Final 1 (1st vs 4th) and Semi-Final 2 (2nd vs 3rd).
- Points: 2 for a win, 1 for tie/no-result, 0 for loss.
- Tiebreaker: Net Run Rate (NRR).

Final Standings (Top 4 qualified):
1. India — 15 points (7 wins, 1 loss, 1 no-result)
2. Australia — 14 points (7 wins, 2 losses)
3. England — 12 points (6 wins, 3 losses)
4. New Zealand — 11 points (5 wins, 3 losses, 1 no-result)
---
5. Pakistan — 11 points (5 wins, 3 losses, 1 no-result) — eliminated on NRR
6. Sri Lanka — 6 points (3 wins, 4 losses, 2 no-results)
7. Bangladesh — 7 points (3 wins, 5 losses, 1 no-result)
8. West Indies — 3 points (2 wins, 7 losses? — need to verify)
9. South Africa — 5 points (3 wins, 5 losses? — eliminated in group stage)
10. Afghanistan — 0 points (0 wins, 9 losses)

Semi-Final Results:
- India vs New Zealand (Old Trafford, Manchester) — New Zealand won by 18 runs (rain delay, played over 2 days)
- Australia vs England (Edgbaston, Birmingham) — England won by 8 wickets (Jason Roy 85, Joe Root 49*)

Final: England vs New Zealand at Lord's — TIED (Super Over also tied) — England won by boundary countback

=== KEY UNUSUAL EVENTS ===

- The boundary countback rule was used for the first and last time in cricket history
- The overthrow off Stokes' bat remains the most controversial moment in WC final history
- Dhoni's run out in the semi-final was his last ever international cricket moment
- Pakistan and New Zealand both had 11 points but Pakistan were eliminated on NRR — the tightest NRR elimination ever
- Rain caused the India vs New Zealand semi-final to be played over 2 days — first time a WC semi-final was a 2-day match
- This was the first 10-team World Cup since 1992 (round-robin format revival)
- Eoin Morgan hit 17 sixes vs Afghanistan — world record for most sixes in an ODI innings
- Controversy over England's 'new approach' to white-ball cricket paying off after their 2015 disaster

=== IMPORTANT CLARIFICATIONS (to prevent common misconceptions) ===

1. The 2019 final was NOT decided by wickets — it was decided by BOUNDARY COUNTBACK after both the match AND Super Over were tied.
2. Rohit Sharma did NOT score a double century (200+) in the 2019 World Cup. His highest score was 140 vs Pakistan. His ODI double centuries (209, 264, 208*) were all scored in bilateral series, NOT in World Cups.
3. Ben Stokes was NOT out when the overthrow hit his bat — he was running between the wickets and the ball deflected accidentally.
4. The Super Over rule was: each team faces 6 balls, team with more runs wins. If tied, boundary countback (now abolished — would be repeated Super Overs).
5. Kane Williamson won Player of the Tournament despite New Zealand losing the final. He scored 578 runs at average 82.57.
