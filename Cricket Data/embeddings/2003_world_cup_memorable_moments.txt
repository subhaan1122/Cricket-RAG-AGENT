ICC Cricket World Cup 2003 — Memorable Moments, Records & Key Events
======================================================================

Tournament: 2003 ICC Cricket World Cup
Host Countries: South Africa, Zimbabwe, Kenya
Dates: 9 February – 23 March 2003
Teams: 14 (Australia, Bangladesh, Canada, England, India, Kenya, Namibia, Netherlands, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, West Indies, Zimbabwe)
Winner: Australia (defeated India in the Final)
Runner-up: India
Player of the Tournament: Sachin Tendulkar
Top Run Scorer: Sachin Tendulkar (673 runs in 11 matches)
Top Wicket Taker: Chaminda Vaas (23 wickets)

=== TOP MEMORABLE MOMENTS ===

1. Sachin Tendulkar's 673-Run Campaign:
   Sachin Tendulkar scored 673 runs in 11 innings at an average of 61.18. He hit one century (98 vs Pakistan was agonizingly close). He scored 152 against Namibia, his highest World Cup score. Tendulkar won the Player of the Tournament award. He was the tournament's leading run scorer by a significant margin.

2. Australia's Unbeaten Run — 11 Wins in 11 Matches:
   Australia won every single match in the 2003 World Cup, going 11-0 through the tournament. This was the first time a team went unbeaten through an entire World Cup. Ricky Ponting led the team with ruthless efficiency. Their winning streak eventually extended to a record-breaking 34 consecutive World Cup wins across multiple tournaments.

3. The 2003 Final — Australia 359/2 vs India 234 all out:
   Date: 23 March 2003. Venue: Wanderers Stadium, Johannesburg.
   Australia batted first and posted 359/2 in 50 overs — the highest score in a World Cup final at that time.
   Ricky Ponting scored a magnificent 140* off 121 balls (4x4s: 4, 8x6s: 4) in one of the greatest World Cup final innings ever.
   Damien Martyn contributed 88* off 84 balls in an unbeaten 234-run partnership with Ponting for the 3rd wicket.
   India were bowled out for 234 in 39.2 overs. Virender Sehwag scored 82 off 81 balls but India collapsed after his dismissal.
   Sachin Tendulkar was dismissed early for 4 runs, caught by Darren Lehmann off Glenn McGrath's bowling.
   Glenn McGrath took 3/52 and was named Player of the Match.
   Australia won by 125 runs, claiming their 3rd World Cup title.
   Turning Point: Tendulkar's early dismissal removed India's biggest threat and demoralized the team.

4. India vs Pakistan — The High-Voltage Clash:
   Date: 1 March 2003. Venue: Centurion, Pretoria.
   India beat Pakistan by 6 wickets in the group stage. Sachin Tendulkar scored 98 off 75 balls, dominating Shoaib Akhtar and Wasim Akram.
   Tendulkar famously upper-cut Shoaib Akhtar for six over third man — one of the most iconic shots in World Cup history.
   India chased down Pakistan's 273/7 with ease, reaching 276/4 in 45.4 overs.

5. Kenya's Fairy-Tale Run to Semi-Finals:
   Kenya became the first non-Test nation to reach a World Cup semi-final.
   They beat Sri Lanka by 53 runs in the group stage, which was a massive upset.
   They received walkovers from New Zealand and Zimbabwe (who refused to play in Kenya for safety/political reasons).
   Kenya eventually lost to India in the semi-final.
   Collins Obuya's leg-spin (5/24 vs Sri Lanka) was one of the performances of the tournament.

6. South Africa's Tragic Exit — The Rain Rule Confusion:
   In the group match vs Sri Lanka at Durban on 3 March 2003, South Africa needed to win to advance.
   Chasing Sri Lanka's 268/9, South Africa's Mark Boucher blocked the last ball with the score at 229/6, not realizing they needed to score one more run.
   Rain had reduced the target via DLS method, and South Africa tied the match when they needed to win.
   Captain Shaun Pollock was devastated. South Africa were eliminated from their home World Cup in heartbreaking fashion.
   This is one of the most painful moments in World Cup history — a host nation eliminated by a mathematical miscalculation.

7. Chaminda Vaas's Hat-Trick — First Ball, Second Ball, Third Ball:
   Date: 19 February 2003. Match: Sri Lanka vs Bangladesh at Pietermaritzburg.
   Chaminda Vaas took a hat-trick with the first three balls of the match — the first hat-trick to start a World Cup match.
   He finished with figures of 6/25 in 9.1 overs, the best bowling figures in the 2003 World Cup.
   Bangladesh were bowled out for just 124. Sri Lanka won by 10 wickets.

8. Andy Bichel's All-Round Heroics:
   Andy Bichel produced one of the greatest all-round performances in World Cup history against England.
   He took 7/20 with the ball (the best bowling figures in the 2003 World Cup after Vaas's 6/25) to bowl England out for 204.
   Then he scored 34* with the bat to guide Australia to victory, hitting the winning runs.

9. Shoaib Akhtar's Raw Pace:
   Shoaib Akhtar bowled the fastest delivery ever recorded in international cricket during the 2003 World Cup — 161.3 km/h (100.2 mph) against England.
   This record stood for many years and remains one of the fastest balls ever bowled.

10. Canada vs Bangladesh — The Associate Upset:
    Date: 11 February 2003. John Davison of Canada scored the fastest century in World Cup history at that time — 67 balls.
    Canada posted 180/7 but Bangladesh chased it down with 3 wickets to spare.

=== RECORDS SET IN 2003 WORLD CUP ===

- Highest team score in a World Cup final: Australia 359/2 vs India (Wanderers, Johannesburg)
- Ricky Ponting 140* — highest individual score in a World Cup final at that time
- Australia's 11-0 unbeaten run through the entire tournament
- Chaminda Vaas hat-trick off first three balls of a match (vs Bangladesh)
- Shoaib Akhtar's 161.3 km/h — fastest delivery in international cricket
- Sachin Tendulkar — 673 runs, Player of the Tournament
- Glenn McGrath 3/52 in the final — key to Australia's dominance
- John Davison (Canada) — 67-ball century, fastest in World Cup at that time
- Andy Bichel 7/20 vs England — second-best bowling figures in 2003 WC

=== MAJOR UPSETS ===

1. Kenya beat Sri Lanka by 53 runs (Group B, 24 Feb 2003)
2. Kenya beat Zimbabwe by 7 wickets (Super Six, 12 March 2003)
3. Zimbabwe beat India by 2 wickets (Group A, 12 Feb 2003) — a rare WC loss for India against an associate/non-Test nation
4. Bangladesh beat Canada (11 Feb 2003)

=== TOURNAMENT FORMAT 2003 ===

Group Stage: 14 teams divided into 2 groups of 7 (Group A and Group B). Each team played every other team in their group once. Top 3 from each group advanced to the Super Six stage.
Super Six: 6 teams played only the 3 teams from the other group (carried forward results against teams from their own group that also qualified). Top 4 advanced to semi-finals.
Semi-Finals: Two semi-finals decided the finalists.
Final: Held at the Wanderers Stadium, Johannesburg on 23 March 2003.
Points: 2 for a win, 1 for a tie/no-result, 0 for a loss.

=== KEY UNUSUAL EVENTS ===

- England and New Zealand refused to play their scheduled matches in Zimbabwe and Kenya respectively, citing safety concerns. They forfeited those matches.
- Shane Warne was sent home before the tournament started after testing positive for a banned diuretic substance.
- Bob Woolmer served as Pakistan's coach during the tournament.
- The tournament was the last World Cup for several legends including Wasim Akram.
