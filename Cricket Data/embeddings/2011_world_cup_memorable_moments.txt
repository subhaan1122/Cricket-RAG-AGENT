ICC Cricket World Cup 2011 — Memorable Moments, Records & Key Events
======================================================================

Tournament: 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup
Host Countries: India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh
Dates: 19 February – 2 April 2011
Teams: 14 (Australia, Bangladesh, Canada, England, India, Ireland, Kenya, Netherlands, New Zealand, Pakistan, South Africa, Sri Lanka, West Indies, Zimbabwe)
Winner: India (defeated Sri Lanka in the Final)
Runner-up: Sri Lanka
Player of the Tournament: Yuvraj Singh
Top Run Scorer: Tillakaratne Dilshan (497 runs)
Top Wicket Taker: Zaheer Khan (21 wickets)

=== TOP MEMORABLE MOMENTS ===

1. India Wins the World Cup After 28 Years:
   India won the 2011 World Cup, their second ODI World Cup title (first since 1983).
   It was the first time a host nation won the World Cup since Australia co-hosted and won in 1992.
   MS Dhoni finished the match with a massive six over long-on — one of the most iconic moments in cricket history.
   The win was especially emotional as it was Sachin Tendulkar's last World Cup — his teammates carried him on their shoulders during the victory lap.

2. The 2011 Final — India 277/4 vs Sri Lanka 274/6:
   Date: 2 April 2011. Venue: Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai.
   Sri Lanka batted first and scored 274/6 in 50 overs.
   Mahela Jayawardene scored a sublime 103* off 88 balls — one of the finest World Cup final innings ever.
   India's chase started badly: Virender Sehwag was out for a duck off the second ball, and Sachin Tendulkar was dismissed for 18.
   India were 31/2 when Gautam Gambhir (97 off 122 balls) and MS Dhoni (91* off 79 balls) rebuilt the innings.
   Gambhir's selfless 97 was arguably the most underrated innings of the final.
   MS Dhoni promoted himself above Yuvraj Singh at No. 5 — a bold captaincy decision that proved decisive.
   Dhoni finished the match with a six off Nuwan Kulasekara to seal the victory. India won by 6 wickets with 10 balls to spare.
   Player of the Match: MS Dhoni.
   Turning Points: (a) Dhoni's decision to bat at No. 5 ahead of Yuvraj, (b) Gambhir's partnership with Dhoni that stabilized the chase after 2 early wickets.

3. Yuvraj Singh's Magnificent All-Round Tournament:
   Yuvraj Singh was named Player of the Tournament.
   He scored 362 runs (average 90.50) and took 15 wickets (average 25.13) — a rare all-round double.
   He scored 4 fifties and took crucial wickets in every knockout match.
   Yuvraj was later diagnosed with a rare germ cell cancer between his lungs and was treated in the US. His World Cup performance became even more remarkable in hindsight.

4. Kevin O'Brien's Fastest World Cup Century — Ireland vs England:
   Date: 2 March 2011. Venue: M Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bangalore.
   Ireland chased down England's 327/8 thanks to Kevin O'Brien's extraordinary 113 off just 63 balls.
   This was the fastest century in World Cup history at that time.
   O'Brien hit 13 fours and 6 sixes. Ireland won by 3 wickets.
   England were humiliated in one of the biggest upsets of the tournament.
   This remains the highest successful run chase by an associate/affiliate nation in World Cup history.

5. India vs Pakistan Semi-Final — The Most Watched Cricket Match Ever:
   Date: 30 March 2011. Venue: Punjab Cricket Association Stadium, Mohali.
   An estimated TV audience of over 1 billion people watched this match.
   India batted first and scored 260/9. Sachin Tendulkar scored 85 and was dropped multiple times by Pakistan fielders (notably Misbah-ul-Haq's side).
   Pakistan were bowled out for 231. Wahab Riaz took 5/46 for Pakistan but it wasn't enough.
   The atmosphere was electric — even Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh and Pakistan PM Yousuf Raza Gilani attended together in a famous "cricket diplomacy" moment.
   Player of the Match: Sachin Tendulkar.

6. Sachin Tendulkar's 2011 World Cup — His Last Dance:
   Tendulkar scored 482 runs in 9 innings at an average of 53.55.
   He scored 2 centuries (120 vs England, 111 vs South Africa).
   After the final win, he famously said: "Tendulkar has carried the weight of the nation on his shoulders for 22 years. It is time we carried him."
   His teammates literally carried him on a victory lap of the Wankhede Stadium.

7. South Africa's Semi-Final Heartbreak (Again):
   South Africa were knocked out in the quarter-finals by New Zealand.
   New Zealand chased down 222 with 6 wickets and 13 balls to spare.
   South Africa's "chokers" label in World Cup knockout matches continued.

8. Virender Sehwag's 175 vs Bangladesh:
   In the opening match of the tournament, Sehwag scored 175 off 140 balls against Bangladesh in Dhaka.
   This was the highest individual score in the 2011 World Cup.
   India won by 87 runs to start their campaign strongly.

9. Strauss Hits Century in Vain as England Collapse:
   Andrew Strauss scored 158 against India, but England still lost by a tight margin in a high-scoring group match.
   The match at Bangalore saw 700+ runs scored.

10. Lasith Malinga's Bowling Masterclass:
    Malinga took 6/38 vs Kenya — the best bowling figures of the 2011 World Cup.
    He was Sri Lanka's strike bowler throughout and took several crucial wickets in knockout stages.

=== RECORDS SET IN 2011 WORLD CUP ===

- Kevin O'Brien 113 off 63 balls — fastest century in World Cup history (at that time)
- Ireland's 329/7 chasing 328 — highest successful chase by an associate nation in World Cup history
- Virender Sehwag 175 — highest individual score in 2011 WC
- Yuvraj Singh — Player of Tournament with 362 runs and 15 wickets (rare double)
- India became the first host nation to win a World Cup since Australia in 1992
- MS Dhoni's winning six in the final became an iconic moment
- Over 1 billion TV viewers for India vs Pakistan semi-final
- Mahela Jayawardene 103* in the final — 4th century in a World Cup final
- Zaheer Khan 21 wickets — leading wicket-taker of the tournament

=== MAJOR UPSETS ===

1. Ireland beat England by 3 wickets (chasing 328, Group B, 2 March 2011) — Kevin O'Brien's 63-ball century
2. Bangladesh beat Ireland by 27 runs (Group B)
3. Kenya never won a match in the tournament
4. Netherlands beat England isn't correct for 2011; but Ireland's chase of 328 was the shock result

=== TOURNAMENT FORMAT 2011 ===

Group Stage: 14 teams divided into 2 groups of 7 (Group A and Group B). Each team played every other team in their group once. Top 4 from each group advanced to the quarter-finals.
Quarter-Finals: 8 teams. A1 vs B4, A2 vs B3, A3 vs B2, A4 vs B1.
Semi-Finals: Winners of quarter-finals. SF1: QF1 winner vs QF2 winner. SF2: QF3 winner vs QF4 winner.
Final: Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai on 2 April 2011.
Points: 2 for a win, 1 for tie/no-result, 0 for loss.

Quarter-Final Results:
- Pakistan beat West Indies by 10 wickets
- India beat Australia by 5 wickets
- Sri Lanka beat England by 10 wickets
- New Zealand beat South Africa by 49 runs

Semi-Final Results:
- India beat Pakistan by 29 runs (Mohali)
- Sri Lanka beat New Zealand by 5 wickets (Colombo)

=== KEY UNUSUAL EVENTS ===

- This was the first World Cup to use DRS (Decision Review System) in some matches
- Indian PM Manmohan Singh and Pakistan PM attended the semi-final together — "cricket diplomacy"
- Sachin Tendulkar's final World Cup — an emotional storyline throughout
- Yuvraj Singh played the tournament unknowingly with cancer
- The tournament was a financial and popular success, especially with India winning on home soil
- No player was given out "handled the ball" in the 2011 World Cup — this is a common misconception
